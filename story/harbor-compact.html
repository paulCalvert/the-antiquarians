Something Below

Ancient. Present. The crew has glimpsed it. They don't have a name for it yet.

What the Crew Has Seen

Traces
  • Something was in the water outside the ghost train windows � vast, dark, not threatening but present
  • The Drowned Ledger responded to it � marine imagery, salt, pressure when near the Reliquary Car
  • It has been there a long time. Maybe even longer than the city.

The Watcher � What It Is

  • An ancient bound entity in Doskvol's harbor � a drowned sentinel transformed into a custodian
  • Its function: keep leviathans and deep horrors out of the bay. Grim and necessary. Not benevolent.
  • It has been doing this job for longer than the current city has existed
  • It is bound by the harbor compact � the six noble seals are its tethers as much as its authority

The Tide Tithe

Public Superstition

Citizens of Doskvol � especially gondoliers, sailors, and dockworkers � throw coins into the harbor water. They call this the Tide Tithe. They don't know why they do it. It's just what you do. This is an unconscious echo of the original compact obligations, passed down through generations as superstition until the meaning was lost. The coins mean nothing to the Watcher. The habit means something.

How It Communicates

  • Not words � images and sensations
  • Marine imagery: the harbor from below, deep water pressure, salt, barnacles
  • Primarily speaks to Costya � she has the clearest connection through the token
  • Also reaches Brick through his delayed reflections � passive attention, not active communication yet
  • Its presence peaks near the Drowned Ledger and in the Sunken Temple beneath the docks

The Metaphysical Contrast

Water vs. Dust

Setarra is dust. The Watcher is water. They are metaphysically opposed. The ghost train's resistance to Setarra's influence came partly from the Watcher's presence running through the route. In the Sunken Temple, the Watcher's influence is strongest � Setarra cannot operate there effectively. This opposition is the key to her eventual defeat.

Current Status

  • Still doing its job � the harbor is currently secure
  • Aware of the crew � noting them, recording them in its own way
  • Showed Costya the stakes during the Sunken Temple score: what the compact working looks like, and what failure looks like
  • If the compact is transferred to Setarra, the Watcher will be compromised � not destroyed, but wrong-commanded

What It Actually Is

  • Created by The Emperor � but the noble bloodlines are the actual signatories
  • Made between The Watcher and six noble houses of Doskvol
  • Governs the harbor's supernatural protection � the Watcher keeps leviathans and deep horrors out
  • The noble signatories are responsible for maintaining and honoring the compact in exchange
  • The Tide Tithe � citizens throwing coins into the water � is an unconscious expression of this obligation, passed down as superstition

The Six Seals

Six house seals are carved into the walls of the inner sanctum and encoded in the Drowned Ledger. Three remain intact — their bloodlines still have living descendants. Three are cracked — those houses have gone extinct and their compact obligations hang unresolved. House Scurlock was never among the six; his house rose and fell in the gap left by a fallen compact family but was never a signatory.

Intact Seals — Three Remaining

House Dalmorrian

A full tree — wide spreading canopy with individually detailed leaves, thick textured trunk, visible surface roots. The ancestor version of the modern Dalmorrian crest; the simplified modern form lost its detail across generations who no longer had a living tree to reference. The stone is warm to Alaric's touch.

Living signatories: Alaric and Jared — same house, same bloodline, one seal. Neither knows it. The Ledger responded to Alaric on the ghost train. His blood opened the inner sanctum door.

Second Intact House — Unnamed

A standing heron, one leg raised, neck curved in the watchful pose — a creature of the shore and the bay margin. A house whose identity was inseparable from the water's edge.

Living signatories: Unknown. The original house name may no longer exist. Centuries of intermarriage, debt, and institutional forgetting scatter bloodlines — descendants could be gentry, tradespeople, clergy, or beggars with no idea what they carry. The compact doesn't require them to know. It only requires the blood.

Third Intact House — Unnamed

A lighthouse tower with a flame at its crown, waves at the base — a beacon marking the harbor mouth. The symbol of civic duty and the protection of those who approach the shore.

Living signatories: Unknown. Same caveat applies — the bloodline survives somewhere in Doskvol, diluted across generations into families that no longer carry the original name or know the original obligation. The compact holds regardless.

Cracked Seals — Three Extinct Lines

Three houses have died out entirely. Their seals are cracked through in the Drowned Ledger. Their obligations are listed as "unresolved" in the compact language — the Watcher still holds his side of the bargain, but no human bloodline remains to fulfill theirs. House Scurlock may have risen into the vacuum left by one of these extinctions. The cracked seals are not yet designed — house identities TBD.

Current Threat

Setarra's Play

Setarra wants to use a living noble signatory to transfer control of the compact to herself. She doesn't need to destroy the system � she needs to own it. With three seals already cracked and the remaining bloodlines in play, she needs only one living signatory to perform the transfer ritual. Jared is the target.

The Ritual Components

Three items are needed to open or transfer the compact: the Drowned Ledger (retrieved), the Sunken Artifact (next score), and a third component (TBD � likely held by a living noble house). Scurlock is directing the crew to collect all three.

Consequences of Failure

  • Setarra gains control � she will not faithfully uphold the compact
  • Gradual maritime collapse: some horrors let through, tides manipulated, fear and instability
  • Not instant destruction � slow, escalating, hard to reverse
  • Campaign 2 premise: the crew must recover the Ledger, repair the compact, and deal with what got through while the Watcher was compromised
