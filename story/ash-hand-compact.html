Tier II — Occult Conspiracy

The Ash-Hand Compact

"What was withheld will be rendered." A dispersed conspiracy of cells serving Setarra’s long game — most of them without knowing the full shape of it.

Harrow spent time inside this network as a mid-level operative. What appears on this page is information he can share with the crew. Sections visible only in GM mode contain details he never had access to.

What They Are

Not a Congregation — A Network

The Ash-Hand Compact is not a single cult with a unified hierarchy. It is a network of cells, each formed around a bargain, a relic, a bloodline, or a leverage point. Most members believe they are pursuing something legitimate: justice, restoration, rightful inheritance, suppressed historical truth. They are lied to constantly. The lies are tailored. They hold.

Their danger is reach and preparation, not brute force. If one cell is dismantled, the network continues. Each cell operates on a "let not one hand know the work of the other" principle — deliberate fragmentation that limits damage from exposure.

Symbols and Marks

An open hand with an eye in the palm, fingers uneven lengths — subtly wrong. Alternately: a closed hand gripping a coin, dust falling through the fingers. Common marks in the field: palm prints in grey ash, five short slash-marks grouped like fingers, wax seals pressed with a palm instead of a signet.

Colors: ash grey, old vellum yellow, faded rust red, soot black. Repeated phrases: "The hand remembers." "A debt delayed is still a debt." "It belongs to the rightful hand." "Open palm, closed bargain." "What is sealed may yet be signed for."

Current Operations

Primary — The Signatory

Veleris Quill’s Meridian Circle front is the active face of this operation. Jared Dalmorrian has been identified as the most accessible living compact signatory — well-meaning, susceptible to prestige, no close family who’d ask uncomfortable questions except Alaric. The goal: maneuver Jared into formalizing a legal transfer of his bloodline’s compact rights. He will not understand what he is signing. That is the point.

Clock: "Jared Commits" — 6 segments, currently at 2.

Secondary — Ritual Components

A separate cell has been working to secure the ritual components independently. They had a cell in the Sunken Temple working the bloodline door for days before the crew arrived. They got the Dirmot equipment. They didn’t get through. Their instructions (recovered from the temple) confirm they knew about the coin and were told not to take it — it is intended for Veleris Quill’s ritual, not for a field cell.

Clock: "Secure Ritual Components" — 8 segments. The crew’s retrieval of the coin sets this back but does not end it.

Tertiary — Alternate Bargains

Setarra has a backup plan for every plan. While the signatory operation runs, the compact is also making smaller contracts across the city: shipping magnates promised favorable tides, a widow promised title restoration, a dock foreman promised a rival crew’s drowning. These surface as clues, complications, and score hooks when the main thread is offscreen.

Faction Structure

Petitioners — Outer Ring

The disposable outer layer. Ruined gamblers, debtors, dock workers, people who “just needed one thing fixed.” They carry messages, watch locations, spread rumors, place shrines. They do not look like cultists. They look like people with something to lose.

Notaries — Middle Ring

The operational backbone. Forgers, occult accountants, ghost clerks, legal fixers. They gather relics, alter records, identify bloodlines, broker bargains, perform minor rites. They feel like clerks who are somehow more dangerous than a thug. Their power is in preparation and paperwork, not confrontation.

Signatories — Inner Ring

True initiates. Senior handlers, broken nobles, deal-victims turned loyalists, people who have physically changed. They oversee major rites, interpret the network’s will, manage cells, prepare pact rituals. When they appear, the air feels wrong. They often have unnaturally dry skin, trembling fingers, shadows with too many hands, voices that double slightly.

Weaknesses

Cell Fragmentation

They do not always coordinate well. One cell may not know what another is doing. A Notary may betray another for personal favor. This creates opportunities for misinformation, partial truths, and turning cells against each other.

They’re Lied To

A lot. Many members think they are pursuing justice, restoration, or rightful claim. When they realize what they are actually feeding, some become redeemable, manipulable, or unstable. Not all — but some.

Ritualists, Not Soldiers

If cornered in a straight fight, most are not terrifying. Their danger is preparation, weird contingencies, cursed leverage, and choosing the battlefield. Get ahead of them and they can be punked.

Water Interference

Setarra is dust. The harbor is water. Their rites degrade near the bay — dust turns to paste, symbols smear, handprints wash away. They are at a structural disadvantage in the Watcher’s domain. The whole conflict has a metaphysical fault line running along this.

Key NPCs

Veleris Quill — Inner Ring, Bloodline Hunter

Runs the Meridian Circle. Currently working Jared. Cold, meticulous, speaks as though everything is already on record. See her NPC page.

Brother Morrow — Inner Ring, Ritual Leader

Former street preacher turned pact zealot. Calm, sorrowful, speaks like an officiant at a legal funeral. One hand wrapped in old legal parchment. Oversees major rites — he is the one who would appear at an endgame ritual, not in early sessions.

Nell Vask — Senior Notary, Field Coordinator

Practical, dangerous, dry humor. Gets things done. Most likely to appear as a recurring rival on scores. Keeps all her contract cuts on the same palm, layered over old scars. Harrow knows her by face and name. She would recognize him — and not know whether he’s still active or long gone.

Pale Jeren — Dockside Petitioner-Broker

Shabby harbor broker who arranges “small bargains” for desperate people. Nervous, always sweating dry somehow. Compulsively rubs grey dust from his fingertips. Good for low-level leads and “someone asked about you” moments. He and Harrow have crossed paths. Jeren would recognize him on the docks.

The Handless Clerk — Unknown Courier

No name. No visible hands — sleeves pinned shut or always hidden. Delivers messages, receipts, and warnings. Every note he delivers smells faintly of dust and old rain. Harrow has received messages from him. He knows him only as the Compact’s courier — nothing beyond the role.

Palm-Mother Edda — Shrine Keeper

Old woman who keeps tiny hand-shrines throughout the city — and below it. Half-prophet, half-homeless nuisance, fully unsettling. Leaves children’s gloves and bent coins. Her shrine appeared in the Sunken Temple’s outer hall. She was there before the cultists. Harrow knows her shrines well. He was trained to recognize and avoid disturbing them.
