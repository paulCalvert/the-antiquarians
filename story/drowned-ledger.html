The Drowned Ledger

It is not treasure. It is a key — and nobody knows quite what it unlocks yet.

What It Is

  • An ancient legal document of some kind — what it governs exactly, the crew doesn't fully understand
  • Seems to be connected to Something Below — the presence in the harbor
  • Contained within a large, ancient, stone-like reliquary/chest — ritual-sealed
  • The binding instrument of the harbor compact — the physical record of an ancient agreement between six noble houses and the Watcher
  • Used to assign, transfer, or renegotiate responsibility for the harbor's protection
  • Contains the six noble seals — three intact, three cracked
  • Heavy with civic-scale supernatural law — whoever holds it is subject to the compact's weight

How It Ended Up on the Ghost Train

  • Was being transported by a noble house — an attempt to move it to the capital
  • Through negligence, it was placed on a normal metro line (the 5th Line) instead of a warded transport
  • This caused a catastrophic metaphysical rejection — the Ledger's power disrupted the mundane infrastructure
  • The 5th Line "broke" — became the ghost train, existing between the material world and the ghost field
  • The Ledger was stuck on the train for decades — unable to be simply removed
  • The city itself seemed to protect it through the train

The Noble Negligence

Who Was Responsible

The nobleman who signed the order to load the Ledger onto the wrong train. His face was obscured in Costya's vision during the escape — but his ring was visible. The crest connects to a bloodline the crew will recognize when the time comes.

Current Status

Now Held By Scurlock

The crew retrieved the Ledger from the ghost train and delivered it to Lord Scurlock. He says it is one of three components needed to contain the threat. The Reliquary is safe — for now.

GM Note

Scurlock intends to give the Ledger to Setarra in exchange for his freedom. The crew believes the opposite. This is one of three components needed for the ritual transfer of the harbor compact — not to contain it.

Properties

  • Responds strangely to certain people — Alaric felt something shift when he entered the Reliquary Car
  • Responds to compact bloodlines specifically
  • Cannot be moved by a thief — whoever takes it must be willing to take on the obligation
  • The Watcher's influence is strongest around the Ledger — marine imagery, pressure, salt
  • Responds strangely to proximity — marine imagery, pressure, the smell of salt in places it shouldn't be
  • Part of a set of three ritual components — alone, it is inert
