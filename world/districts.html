Districts in Focus

The neighborhoods the crew actually moves through — what they feel like from the street.

Nightmarket

The Crew's Home Ground

Nightmarket is named for its running bazaars — stalls and storefronts that operate around the clock, catering to the city's eternal night schedule. It sits near the rail station, which makes it a hub of goods and people coming in from outside Doskvol. The district has money — not noble money, but merchant money: import traders, wholesalers, specialty craftspeople, and the middlemen who connect all of them. The antique shop sits in a block of similar small commerce, which means unusual foot traffic and unusual visitors attract little notice. Nobody on this street is there by accident, and everybody minds their own business.

The Social Texture

Nightmarket is one of the more culturally mixed districts in the city — the commercial pull draws people from every background. Iruvian spice merchants operate two blocks from Skovlan textile traders. The result is a neighborhood that has learned to be tolerant of difference in the same way commercial environments always do: because exclusion is bad for business. It is not a warm tolerance. It is a functional one. The district has no dominant criminal faction — the Hive has interests here but does not control it outright, which is part of why the crew's base here works. Nobody owns this block. That is an advantage.

The Rail Station

Doskvol's rail station is the terminus for the overland coach-and-rail network that connects the city to the rest of the Imperium. Cargo comes in daily — legitimate goods, contraband, people who need to arrive without using the normal docks, and information moving between cities. The station itself is heavily watched: customs officials, Bluecoat patrols, and Ministry of Preservation inspectors checking outgoing leviathan products. The area around the station — warehouses, freight handlers, cheap lodgings for travelers — is considerably less watched and considerably more useful.

Crow's Foot

Gang Crossroads

Crow's Foot is where the city's criminal gravity concentrates. Several gang territories overlap here without any single faction dominating, which means the district is in a permanent low-level state of competition that occasionally spikes into open violence. The streets are narrow, the buildings close, and the population density high — everyone who lives here knows their neighbors and is known by them in turn. This makes it difficult to operate anonymously. It also means information moves fast, alliances form quickly, and any crew operating here needs to be legible to the existing social order or be treated as a threat.

Current State

The Crows' leadership transition — Lyssa killing Roric and taking his chair — has destabilized the district's existing balance. The Lampblacks and Red Sashes are both maneuvering for advantage in the vacuum. The Hive is watching with open interest, having not forgotten what happened to Roric. For the Antiquarians, Crow's Foot is a place to take meetings and run scores, not to establish roots — too much flux, too many factions watching each other, too easy to get caught between competing interests on a bad night.

Mad Eye Jake operates somewhere in the Crow's Foot area. He has reasons to remember the Antiquarians specifically. This is relevant when the crew has business in the district.

Silkshore

The Pleasure District

Silkshore runs along the water — canals, gondola landings, the decorated facades of pleasure houses and gambling establishments reflected in dark water. It has a reputation for romance that it has cultivated carefully: the gondola rides at night, the lit windows of establishments that cater to every appetite, the artists and musicians who perform in its streets. The reputation is accurate as far as it goes. What it omits is that Silkshore's primary industry is the management of desire, and like any industry, it has supply chains, labor arrangements, and power structures that do not appear in the promotional material.

Who Really Runs It

Silkshore's establishments are owned by a mixture of independent proprietors, faction-backed operations, and holdings tied to noble houses through layers of intermediaries. The district has its own informal governance through a council of establishment owners — not official, not acknowledged, but functional. They manage the peace between factions well enough that Silkshore has avoided the overt violence of Crow's Foot. Wealthy clients require discretion and stability; disruption is bad for everyone's revenue. This makes Silkshore unusually well-policed by its own interests. The Watch presence here is minimal and largely decorative.

A Scurlock Property

One of Scurlock's more visible holdings sits in Silkshore — visible from the water, older than most of the surrounding buildings by several centuries, and architecturally inconsistent with everything around it in ways that suggest it predates the district. It operates as a place of business and discrete meetings, staffed and maintained to a standard the neighbors have learned not to inquire about. It is not where he lives. It is where he receives people he does not want to receive at home.

Six Towers

The Abandoned District

Six Towers is a district in long decline — the noble families whose towers once anchored it have consolidated into Brightstone and Whitecrown, leaving behind crumbling stone, squatters in the upper floors, and streets that the Watch patrols infrequently and the gangs haven't fully claimed because there isn't enough here to claim. The towers themselves still stand: six lightning barrier structures ringing the district, all in poor repair, all abandoned. The district around them has followed the towers down. What's left is cheap housing, ghost activity in the ruins, and the kind of quiet that comes from everyone with options having already left.

Lord Scurlock's Residence

Scurlock lives in Six Towers — not in one of the towers themselves, but in one of the old manors that once surrounded them, now standing in the shadow of crumbling stone. The manor is in better repair than its neighbors, which is not saying much. It is not where he entertains. It is where he actually is. The district suits him: old, mostly abandoned, and full of people who have learned that the best policy regarding their neighbors is indifference. Nobody asks what he does there. Nobody has for a long time.

He has properties and investments throughout the city — Silkshore, Charterhall, the docks. But this is where he comes back to. The district's decay doesn't bother him. He has watched places much better than this fall much further.

Brightstone

The Noble District

Brightstone is the most visible district in Doskvol — the one that appears in Imperial propaganda, the one that visiting dignitaries are taken to, the one that the city's ruling class has designed to reflect their self-image back at them. Grand stone buildings with ornamental facades. Wide boulevards maintained by city workers who operate only here. Manicured parks lit by electroplasmic lamps of unusual quality. The Sanctorium's tower visible from most of the district. It is genuinely impressive. It is also maintained at enormous expense partly drawn from the misery of Dunslough and Charhollow, a fact that the architecture is not designed to communicate.

Operating Here

The Bluecoats in Brightstone actually function as law enforcement — Commander Bowmore's influence means this district is patrolled by Watch officers who are not simply extorting the locals. For the crew, this means that the usual tools for managing Watch attention (bribes, informal arrangements) are less reliable here. What works in Brightstone is social cover: looking like you belong, knowing whose party you're supposed to be at, having a name that opens the right door. Being visibly out of place in Brightstone draws attention faster and more effectively than in any other district. The Watch here is asking why you're here, not how much you can pay.

The Social Map

Brightstone's events — dinners, Academy functions, Sanctorium services, private gatherings at noble estates — are where alliances are made, broken, and managed. The Bowmore-Clelland-Rowan faction holds most of the social momentum right now. Strangford's people attend but feel the chill. Dunvil plays both sides. Penderyn is present but operates on his own frequency. The minor gentry — including House Dalmorrian and House Quellin — circulate as observers and occasionally as useful neutrals. Information from these rooms is extremely valuable. Access to these rooms requires either the right name or someone who has it and is willing to vouch for you.

The Undercity

Below the Streets

Beneath Doskvol's districts lies an older layer: pre-Cataclysm foundations, flooded tunnels, collapsed structures, and canal infrastructure that predates the city above it. The undercity is not a single place — it is a patchwork of accessible spaces varying by district and depth. Some of it is actively used: smuggling routes, hidden passages between buildings, the approach to the Sunken Temple beneath the Docks. Some of it is simply there, waiting, accumulating spirit energy in the dark. The ghost field density underground is significantly higher than street level. Things have had a long time to settle here.
