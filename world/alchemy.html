Alchemy — Philosophy of the Craft

The Ghost Field Problem

In the world before the Cataclysm, chemistry and the arcane were considered separate disciplines. In Doskvol, that separation doesn't fully hold. The ghost field permeates everything — stone, water, the air in a sealed room. At the margins of any sufficiently complex chemical process, the ether bleeds in. A reaction that should be predictable produces unexpected results near a strong ghost presence. A drug that works reliably in Brightstone behaves differently in a haunted canal district. Every Leech learns this early: you are not working in a controlled environment. You are never working in a controlled environment.

Transmutation

Technically possible. Practically unreliable. The ghost field introduces enough variability that controlled transmutation — reliable conversion of one substance into another — is extremely difficult outside of laboratory conditions that most practitioners cannot maintain. What exists instead are one-off successes, partial transformations, and a significant body of accident reports. The Academy maintains records of successful transmutation experiments going back two centuries. Almost none have been reproduced exactly.

The practical limit isn't the theory — it's the consistency. A Leech who achieves transmutation has achieved it once. Replicating it requires either perfect conditions or a deeper understanding of why it worked, which most practitioners don't have time to develop between paying jobs.

Immortality — The Expensive Question

Alchemists do not officially pursue the elixir of life. The Academy's position is that this falls outside sanctioned research and into the domain of forbidden arcane practice. Unofficially, it is the persistent obsession behind a significant portion of advanced alchemical work — approached sideways: extending lifespan through chemistry, reducing biological degradation, working on the symptoms rather than claiming to pursue the thing itself.

What practitioners know: something in this direction exists, because there are people in Doskvol who are older than they should be. How they got there and what it cost them is not a question most are willing to answer directly.

How Leeches Learn

Three paths. Academy training is formal, credential-granting, and deliberately incomplete — the most dangerous knowledge is not in the curriculum. Apprenticeship to a practicing Leech is faster and more practical, but depends entirely on who your master is and what they're willing to share. Self-teaching from archived texts is the third path: slower, riskier, and the source of most of the field's interesting accidents. Most working Leeches have used all three at different points. The knowledge doesn't stay in one place.

Demonic Consultation

It happens. Demons know things about transmutation, the ghost field, and biological chemistry that no living scholar has independently derived — they've had longer to work on it. The practice is an open secret in the upper tier of the Academy and among the most capable independent practitioners. It is also deeply illegal, religiously condemned, and personally dangerous in ways that go beyond the obvious.

The risk isn't just possession or corruption. A demon consulted for alchemical knowledge will give accurate information. It will also contextualize that information in ways that shift how the practitioner thinks about what they're doing. The danger is cumulative and hard to notice from inside it. Victor is not there yet. Whether he gets there is a question worth watching.
