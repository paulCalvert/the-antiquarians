The Ghost Field

A second layer of reality over the physical world � present everywhere, visible only to those trained to look.

What It Is

Not a Separate Dimension

The ghost field is not somewhere else � it exists in the same physical space as the city, layered directly on top of it. Those who perceive it describe it as a copy of reality overlaid on the normal world: bluish, desaturated, slightly distorted. Like a second image pressed against the first. It is present everywhere, all the time. Most people cannot perceive it at all. Whispers can open their minds to it. The result is occupying both layers simultaneously.

Why It's Dense Now

Roughly 850 years ago the Cataclysm broke the Gates of Death � the metaphysical boundary that allowed spirits to pass from the living world to whatever lay beyond. The result: spirits stopped passing on. They accumulated instead. After eight centuries of this, the ether is at what scholars call "high tide" � denser with spirit energy than at any prior point in recorded history. Electroplasm sometimes condenses out of the air in high-density areas. Spirit wells form spontaneously. The dead rise more easily. The ghost field is more present now than it has ever been.

Ghost Types

  • Echo � The most common type. Caught in a behavioral loop, mindlessly re-enacting actions or routines from life. No conscious will, no agenda. Still dangerous � echoes can discharge electroplasm unpredictably. Most haunted buildings involve echoes.
  • Specter � Conscious and malevolent. Has will, intent, and typically an active agenda � revenge, possession, unfinished business turned dark. Specters can attempt to possess the living to drain life essence or exact vengeance. The most dangerous common ghost type.
  • Horror � Non-human electroplasmic entities. Not human spirits � creatures of the ether, or spirits so degraded they have lost all humanity. Most common in the deathlands beyond the barrier. The lightning towers exist largely to keep these out.
  • Reconciled � Extremely rare. A sane, stable ghost that has found a way to persist without degenerating. Retains personality, memory, and reason. The faction called the Reconciled is an association of these spirits � they exist in an uncomfortable limbo, recognized fully by neither the living nor the dead. The Spirit Wardens consider them a priority target regardless.
  • Spirit Wells � Not a ghost per se, but a geographic phenomenon: a rift or convergence point where the ghost field is extraordinarily dense, attracting spirits and supernatural energy. Spirit wells act as nodes of power and danger � and as amplifiers for anything occult happening nearby.

What Whispers Perceive

Attunement

Through Attunement, a Whisper opens their mind to the spirit layer. While attuned they perceive: invisible or dormant ghosts not yet manifested to ordinary senses; spiritual residue at sites of death, trauma, or arcane activity � a stain left on a location that can last decades; electroplasmic wards, spirit traps, and enchantments; the emotional and memory echo of places; demons and horrors that are invisible or disguised in the physical world.

A spirit mask functions as precision optics for the ghost field � finer perception and some protection against spectral harm. Only through a spirit mask can ghost doors be seen. Without it, Attunement gives broad awareness; with it, detail and navigation become possible.

Ghost Doors

Passages Through the Ether

Ghost doors form at sites of death. More violent or traumatic deaths create longer-lasting doors � ranging from a few hours to permanent apertures that have been open for decades. They allow rapid non-physical travel between two points in the city; entering one allows traversal of distance that would take far longer through the streets.

Ghost doors are only visible to someone attuned with a spirit mask. Traversal requires focused effort and carries real risk: nightmares, unwanted companions following you back from the other side, truths about the deaths that formed the door becoming involuntarily visible. You can only navigate between ghost doors you have already seen in the physical world � entering a door without knowing what's on the other side leads somewhere unknown.

The Death Cycle

What Happens When Someone Dies in Doskvol

The death bells at Bellweather Crematorium toll � audible only to those near the death and to Spirit Wardens through their arcane masks. Deathseeker crows launch from the crematorium's eaves and circle above the body's location in the sky. Spirit Warden teams deploy immediately with carts to recover the body. At the crematorium, the body is incinerated in electroplasmic furnaces � destroying the ghost utterly and permanently, not just the body but the spirit. If the body is not recovered within roughly three days, the spirit detaches from the corpse and manifests on its own.

The system exists to prevent ghost accumulation in the city. Without it, Doskvol would be uninhabitable. The Spirit Wardens' efficiency is why the city is livable at all � and why the criminal underworld will pay well to intercept a body before they arrive.

Electroplasm

Compressed, processed life energy � the physical form that spiritual energy takes when extracted or refined. Raw leviathan blood is the primary source material. Processed electroplasm powers the lightning barriers, street lamps, spark-craft technology, and radiant energy growing operations. Spirit essence � distilled from ghosts � is a controlled substance, wildly illegal to produce without imperial authorization.

The relationship between spirit energy and electricity is not metaphorical in this world � they are the same substance in different states. The lightning barriers work because they're running spiritual energy powerful enough to repel ghosts and horrors. The ghost field and the city's industrial power grid are made of the same stuff.

Rituals

Rituals are learned in downtime through research or recovered as score payoffs � each requires completing an 8-segment progress clock before it can be performed. Performing a ritual takes at least one downtime activity (sometimes more for large-scale effects), inflicts stress proportional to its magnitude, and may require an Attune roll if the outcome is dangerous or uncertain. Longer preparation can reduce the stress cost. These are not spells cast in a moment � they are deliberate, costly, and leave a mark.

Tracking & Divination

Ghost Map � 4 Stress

Enchants a specially prepared map to reveal the positions of spirits across an entire district. Active manifestations, echo loops, specter territories � all visible as long as the map is maintained. Requires a clean map, at least two hours of sustained attunement, and a drop of the caster's blood to anchor it. The spirits shown cannot tell they are being observed. One of two official ritual examples from the rulebook.

Death Mark � 2 Stress

Place an invisible attunement mark on a living person. The moment they die, the mark fires like a flare in the ghost field � the caster knows instantly, and knows exactly where the ghost manifests. Lasts until death or until deliberately removed. Useful for monitoring a target, reaching a body before the Spirit Wardens, or confirming an enemy's end. The mark is detectable by other attuned practitioners who examine the target closely.

The Long Read � 1�4 Stress

Enter a sustained attunement with a specific object and experience its spiritual history � every significant event that left an impression, played back in sequence. A knife that killed someone. A chair where a person grieved for years. A coin that passed through a thousand hands. The caster is a passive observer: they receive what the object witnessed, they cannot direct it or skip forward. Cost scales with the weight of what's experienced. A mundane object: 1 stress. A murder weapon or ritual implement from a significant death: 3 or 4. Traumatic histories cause trauma.

Communion & Contact

Speak Through the Glass � 3 Stress

Establish two-way communication with a specific known ghost. Requires a personal possession of theirs, knowledge of their name, or a visit to where they died. The ghost must be willing to engage � compelled ghosts answer in half-truths and deflections. Reconciled ghosts are the most useful conversationalists, and will ask for something in return. Each conversation gives the ghost slightly more purchase on the caster: they learn your patterns, your fears, your weak moments. Repeated contact with a specter is strongly inadvisable for this reason.

Portal to the Depths � 6 Stress, Desperate Roll

Opens a gateway in the ghost field, anchored at a point up to a few blocks away, that reaches down to the Void Sea. Anything near the portal is pulled toward it. Through it, a Whisper can commune directly with a leviathan � the creature's dreaming mind is accessible, and it will respond, after a fashion. What it says and in what form is unpredictable. The leviathan does not experience time the way you do. One of two official ritual examples from the rulebook.

Clock: "Seduced by the Leviathan's Song" (4 segments). Each communion adds a tick. When the clock fills, the leviathan has decided it wants to keep you.

Demonic Consultation � 4+ Stress

Invoke a demon's name within a prepared circle of black sand and crematory ash to bind it temporarily to the physical world for questioning. Demons are ancient, patient, and extremely specific about what they are and are not obligated to tell you. The circle holds them for as long as the ritual holds. What you learn depends entirely on what you ask and whether the demon considers the exchange worthwhile. Cost scales with the demon's tier. The demon remembers the encounter. So do you � whether you want to or not.

GM Note � Demonic Consultation & Setarra

Attempting this ritual to contact or question Setarra directly would be extraordinarily dangerous � she is distributed, she is aware, and she would notice. A Whisper attempting this should be warned in-world beforehand (the circle's behavior changes when her name is invoked), not simply punished. It is a valid thing to attempt; it should not be a casual one.

Binding & Containment

Soul Cage � 2 Stress + Materials

Craft a spirit bottle capable of trapping and containing a ghost. The container can be any vessel that has been properly warded and inscribed � a glass bottle, a carved box, a locket. This ritual prepares the vessel only; capturing a ghost requires a separate action. The bottle's capacity is limited by its quality: a ghost of sufficient power will break cheap work. Spirit Wardens use industrial versions of this process. Producing spirit essence from a captured ghost is wildly illegal and will eventually attract Warden attention.

Spirit Anchor � 3 Stress

Bind a ghost to a specific location or object, preventing it from moving beyond a defined perimeter. The ghost knows it is bound. An echo will simply continue its loop within the space; a specter will test every edge of the containment looking for weaknesses. The anchor must be physically maintained � a circle of inscriptions, a buried token, a ward on the threshold. If the physical anchor is disrupted, the binding ends instantly. Can be used to contain a dangerous ghost, preserve a spirit for later questioning, or post a guard that never sleeps and never needs to be paid.

Warding & Defense

Ectoplasmic Ward � 2 Stress per Area

Inscribe a space with warding script that creates a barrier ghosts cannot cross. The ward is physically present in the ghost field as a solid boundary. Echoes are stopped entirely; specters are repelled with effort; horrors can force through but take significant harm doing so. Does not prevent the possession of someone already inside the warded space. Duration: until physically disrupted. An attuned practitioner examining the space can see the inscriptions clearly and knows exactly what they are.

Ghost Ward � 3 Stress

Ward a specific person against the ghosts of those they have wronged or killed � preventing vengeful spirits from finding or reaching them. Performance: prepare a mixture of tobacco, dream smoke, and crematory ash from one of their victims; the target smokes it. As long as the ward holds, the target is invisible to their victim-ghosts. Side effect: the warded person sometimes weeps tears of black blood in their sleep, especially near significant emotional resonance. The ward may provoke retaliatory attention from the spirits � toward the caster rather than the target. Official worked example from the rulebook.

High Risk

The Invitation � 3 Stress

Willingly lower your spiritual defenses and invite a known, trusted ghost to share your body for a period. Not possession � the ghost is a passenger, not the driver. They experience the physical world through you simultaneously; the caster benefits from their knowledge, memories, and perception. The ghost must consent. This is the safest form of voluntary co-habitation. It is not safe.

Clock: "Reluctant to Leave" (4 segments). Each session of co-habitation adds a tick. When the clock fills, the ghost has grown too comfortable to leave willingly and must be forcibly removed.

How Doskvol Lives With the Dead

The ghost field is a fact of life in Doskvol. What it means — and what should be done about it — depends entirely on who you ask. These are not minor differences of opinion. They are deep philosophical divides that shape how factions operate, what they destroy, and what they refuse to touch.

The Relational Approach — Whispers, Gondoliers

The ghost field is not a problem. It is a presence — a second layer of reality that has its own logic, its own inhabitants, and its own obligations. Ghosts were people. The spirit world is continuous with the living one, not separate from it. You engage with it the way you engage with anything that has its own will: with attention, care, and a willingness to understand before you act.

A Whisper practicing this approach doesn't ask how do I eliminate this ghost. They ask why is this ghost here. The answer tells you more than destruction ever could — and sometimes the answer means the spirit doesn't need to be eliminated at all.

The Gondoliers embody this institutionally. They navigate the canals every day — liminal spaces where the living city and the spirit world press closest together. They have working relationships with things that haunt the waterways, built over generations. Not friendship. Not worship. Working relationships. They know what the harbor remembers because they've been listening.

Harrow's specific position: the ghost field is not alien terrain. It is home territory that most people have simply been trained to fear. The Spirit Wardens' habit of destroying everything they don't understand doesn't protect the city — it just makes the city's relationship with its own dead worse.

The Suppressive Approach — Spirit Wardens

The ghost field is infrastructure that has catastrophically failed. Eight hundred years ago the Gates of Death broke and the dead stopped passing on. That is not a mystery to be contemplated — it is a crisis that has to be managed, every single day, or the city becomes uninhabitable. Contain. Process. Destroy. The system works because they do this job.

They are not wrong that management is necessary. The city would drown in accumulated spirits without them. Their error is a slower one: triage applied long enough becomes medicine. They have been in continuous emergency response for so long that the emergency is now the baseline, and the question of whether there is a better relationship with the dead has stopped being a question they ask.

The City Must Survive. Individual spirits, individual suffering, individual ghosts who might have been communicated with — all of it is acceptable collateral in service of that larger function. They are not cruel. They are optimized for scale in a way that leaves no room for anything smaller than the city itself.

What they miss: every ghost they process was carrying something — a memory, a grievance, information about what happened to them and why. Destruction as default response destroys all of that. The city's relationship with its own history is impoverished partly because of how thoroughly they do their job.

The Extractive Approach — Spark Wrights, Leeches

The ghost field is a natural resource. Electroplasm is energy. Spirits are made of something — something measurable, something that obeys consistent rules, something that can be studied and put to work. This is not a mystical question or a moral one. It is an engineering question.

Where Whispers commune and Spirit Wardens destroy, Leeches engineer. They want to understand the mechanics: what ghosts are made of, how electroplasm behaves under different conditions, what rules govern spirit manifestation. Understanding those rules means you can do something with them — build better containment, produce better materials, design systems that actually account for the second layer of reality rather than pretending it isn't there.

The ghost field is like weather or tidal patterns. A vast natural force that doesn't care about you. The smart move isn't to commune with it or suppress it — it's to understand it well enough to stop being its victim and start being its operator.

The tension with the relational approach: extraction treats spirits as material. A Whisper who believes a ghost was a person finds this somewhere between uncomfortable and monstrous. The tension with the suppressive approach: Leeches sometimes keep ghosts around for study, which Spirit Wardens consider recklessly dangerous — and which, to be fair, it often is.

Cults and the Ghost Field

Why Cults Form

The ghost field is full of things with power — old entities, bound demons, spirits of unusual strength, and fragments of pre-Cataclysm obligations that still carry weight in the ether. Some of these things can hear you. Some of them will answer. Cults form around that fact, always have, and the Spirit Wardens' efforts to suppress them have never fully succeeded because the ghost field itself cannot be switched off.

Most cults are small, fragmented, and short-lived. A cell forms around a shared bargain or a single charismatic figure with a connection to something in the ether. When the bargain comes due or the figure dies, the cell scatters. What replaces it is rarely organized — splinters, survivors, people who were touched by something and didn't stop looking for it.

Shrines

Small shrines appear throughout Doskvol in the margins of daily life — tucked into dock posts, chalked onto canal walls, wedged into the gaps in old stone. Bent coins, carved figures, ash handprints, barnacle-covered handbells. Most of them honor nothing in particular: they are folk behavior, the accumulated superstition of people who live close to the ghost field and feel its presence without understanding it. Some of them are dedicated to something specific. The difference is not always obvious from the outside.

The Spirit Wardens remove the ones they find. The ones they find are a fraction of what exists.

Setarra

Among Doskvol's population there are people who have already made arrangements with the demon of dust and deals. Not all of them would describe themselves as worshippers — most would say they made a practical decision in a difficult moment, and that they fully intend to fulfill their end. What they share is that she has not forgotten them, and the arrangements are still active. Some have gathered around that shared condition. They don't always know each other. They don't need to. The arrangements are the congregation.

GM — The Obligation Approach (Ancient, Nearly Forgotten)

There is a fourth relationship with the ghost field that almost nobody in the current city understands: the ghost field has obligations woven into it. Not metaphors. Actual bindings — legal structures, written into the ether itself, that pre-date the current city and will outlast it.

The harbor compact is the most significant surviving example. The Watcher's existence and function are inscribed into the ghost field as obligation, not just as a fact of nature. The six noble houses didn't simply acknowledge him — their bloodlines became part of the field's structure through the compact. The Drowned Ledger isn't a document that describes the compact. It is the compact, written in a language the ghost field reads.

This approach doesn't align with any current faction. The Spirit Wardens have records of ancient compacts but no framework for understanding their significance. The Gondoliers sense the obligation structure through the water — it informs how they move and what they avoid — but they couldn't articulate it as a philosophy. Whispers can sometimes feel the edges of it when attuned near sites like the temple, but most would describe it as "something old and heavy" rather than a legal structure.

The relevant implication: Setarra's play is not primarily a supernatural attack. It is a legal maneuver. She is trying to amend the compact — to have a living signatory transfer its obligations to her. If she succeeds, the ghost field's own structure enforces her authority over the harbor. You cannot fight that with containment or communion or engineering. You have to understand the obligation itself.
