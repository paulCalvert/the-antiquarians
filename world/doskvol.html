The City of Doskvol

Eleven districts. Eternal dark. A civilization built on leviathan blood, ghost energy, and borrowed time.

The Eternal Dark

The Sun Barely Exists

The Cataclysm nearly extinguished the sun. It appears now only as a tiny ember briefly visible at dawn and dusk � not enough to illuminate the city, not enough to grow crops, not enough to mark reliable time. Doskvol runs on electroplasmic street lamps, radiant energy growing lamps, fire, and coal. The city is never fully dark � the lightning towers at the perimeter glow constantly, and electroplasmic light pools from windows and lamp posts across every district. But it is always night.

The Blind Hours

Thick fog blankets Doskvol at dawn and dusk. By long custom, citizens stay indoors during this period. The fog is when the worst spiritual activity occurs � and, relatedly, when most criminal operations prefer to run. The Blind Hours are not law. They are habit, and habit in this city has usually been earned through painful experience.

The Districts

Whitecrown

The seat of power. The Lord Governor's stronghold, Doskvol Academy (elite military and leviathan hunter training), the Master Warden's estate. Heavily guarded. Most ordinary citizens have never crossed the bridge to Whitecrown.

Brightstone

The mansions and marble. Grand houses of the wealthy, manicured parks, the Sanctorium cathedral. Home to the City Council families. The most policed district in the city by competent Bluecoats. Fine dining, cultural events, elaborate dress codes. The stage where the nobility perform power at each other.

Charterhall

The bureaucratic heart. Civic offices, banks, legal firms, Doskvol University, government records. The professional middle class. Comfortable, regulated, and the place where paper trails get buried.

Silkshore

The pleasure district. Gondolas, pleasure houses, bohemian artists, gambling. The most atmospheric and dangerous district for a certain class of vice. Romanticized in popular culture.

Nightmarket

New money. Commerce hub near the rail station. Merchants and traders who have accumulated wealth without acquiring noble titles. Social tension between new money and old nobility runs visibly here.

Crow's Foot

Gang crossroads. A patchwork of competing territories and street violence. Currently controlled by the Crows. Dense, dangerous, and full of opportunity for those who know how to navigate it.

Charhollow

The tenements. A maze of cramped row houses and squalid housing for laborers. Communities here are bound by mutual care and collective survival � the most coherent working-class neighborhoods in the city. Not safe. Safer than Dunslough.

Coalridge

The factory district. Soot, noise, factories, industrial workhouses. Where things are made. The air is bad. The hours are worse. Several gangs compete for control of the workforce and the product.

The Docks

Ancient port. Leviathan hunter ships, cargo trade, fishing, taverns. Predates the Cataclysm. Rough culture. The Dockers have real labor power here � they have leveraged it before and are not shy about doing so again.

Six Towers

Decay and shadow. Formerly the prestigious district of noble towers — now entirely abandoned, converted to cheap apartments, or outright ruins. The noble families whose names these towers once carried have long since consolidated into manor estates in Brightstone and Whitecrown. The towers themselves stand largely empty: crumbling stone, collapsed wings, disputed squatter claims. Haunted. Criminal. Full of ghost activity in the ruins.

Barrowcleft

Rural farms. Doskvol's food belt � crops grown under radiant energy lamps. Tight-knit communities, suspicious of outsiders, and under Ministry of Preservation oversight for most of what they produce.

Dunslough

The bottom. Site of Ironhook Prison. The destitute, the imprisoned, the forgotten. The Weeping Lady charity maintains the only real social support here. Adjacent to industrial pollution runoff. Where people end up when they have run out of other options.

Daily Life

What People Eat

The common diet is eel (plentiful in the canals, resistant to pollution), mushrooms (grown in tunnels, closets, and basements throughout the city), watermoss, and canal weed. Eel pies and mushroom bread are the standard working-class meal. The wealthy eat imported seafood arranged to resemble living creatures, fresh produce from radiant-energy gardens, and goat cheese. About half the city's food is imported; the other half grown locally under artificial light. The Ministry of Darkbloom maintains public mushroom gardens. Citizens are accustomed to eating food that has never seen sunlight.

The Doskvol Calendar � Six months of 60 days each, divided into ten six-day weeks per month. Months: Mendar, Kalivet, Suran, Ulsivet, Volnivet, Elisar. Days are numbered, not named, though districts have their own customs for market and rest days. Major holidays: Moontide (last day of each month � informal heavy drinking), Unison (commemorates the end of the Unity War with Skovlan � politically charged), Gratitude (honors the Immortal Emperor's ascension).

Class Structure

The Tiers

Noble houses � Six Council families, plus a tier of lesser gentry with titles but no seats. Old money, old names, old towers. Wealthy elite � industrial magnates, leviathan hunter officers, senior Sparkwrights. Have money but not necessarily blood. Professional class � magistrates, bank clerks, guild officials, senior artisans, academics. Comfortable, careful, and invested in the system working. Working class � dockers, factory hands, fishermen, canal workers. Organized in pockets (Dockers have power; most workers have none). Destitute � Dunslough, canal squatters, the permanently unemployed. Ironhook looms. The criminal class cuts across all other tiers � the only social mobility that moves reliably in both directions.

What It Costs to Be Poor Here

Poverty in Doskvol has a specific texture that makes it worse than poverty in a normal city. Food cannot be grown outdoors � the eternal dark and the deathlands make subsistence farming impossible. Everything edible is either produced in artificial growing operations (expensive infrastructure, controlled by the Ministry of Preservation) or imported through the docks (taxed, managed, and priced by factors who have no incentive to keep it affordable). A working-class family in Charhollow spends a higher proportion of their income on food than any equivalent household would in a city with agriculture. Eel and mushrooms are cheap because they grow in the margins � the canals and the dark spaces. Everything else is priced for the people who can afford it.

Debt as Control

The dominant mechanism of working-class life in Doskvol is debt. Factory owners provide company housing and company stores at rates that ensure wages are perpetually insufficient to clear what workers owe. Landlords in Charhollow operate on rolling credit that resets faster than it can be paid down. The Bluecoats in poor districts are not law enforcement � they are a debt collection apparatus for whoever is currently paying them. The professional class understands this and is careful to stay on the right side of it. The noble class created it. The criminal world offers a sideways exit: the possibility of acquiring enough coin fast enough to break out of the cycle. This is why criminal recruitment in Charhollow and Dunslough is never difficult. The alternative is visible every day from the tenement window.

The Weeping Lady charity in Dunslough and Charhollow operates the only free food distribution in the city. The Church of the Ecstasy makes charitable donations to it annually. The Church's contributions cover roughly 8% of the operation's costs. The rest comes from a mix of working-class mutual aid, the occasional sympathetic merchant, and sources the organizers do not discuss.

Religion

Church of the Ecstasy of the Flesh

The state religion. Public doctrine honors bodily life and abhors corrupted spirits. The grand Sanctorium in Brightstone is the center of civic religious life � attendance is expected for the upper classes, and its charitable arms maintain some presence in the poorer districts. The Church teaches that the body is the seat of divine experience, and that the spirit's place is beyond � not lingering, not accumulated, not bottled. This puts it in philosophical alignment with the Spirit Wardens, if not always in practical cooperation.

The Weeping Lady � a faith and charity organization honoring Lady Devera, a historical champion of the poor. Active in Dunslough and Charhollow. Not affiliated with the Church of the Ecstasy. The closest thing Doskvol has to a street-level religious institution that actually serves the people who need it.
