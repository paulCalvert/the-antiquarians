Law & Enforcement

The systems that maintain Doskvol's order — and who they actually serve.

The Bluecoats

The City Watch

Officially responsible for street patrol, public order, and operating Ironhook Prison. In practice, accurately described by those who know them as another street gang extorting the underclasses. The Bluecoats serve the richest and most influential, harass the poorest, and operate under Commander Arvus Clelland — Chief Commissioner, House Clelland — who is corrupt, cruel, and dismissive. This tone starts at the top and filters down through every precinct in the city.

District Variation

Bluecoats in Brightstone behave differently from those in Crow's Foot or Charhollow. In Brightstone, Commander Bowmore's presence means the Watch functions closer to actual law enforcement — protecting real property for real people who will notice if it isn't. Everywhere else, bribery is not a workaround. It is the system. Warrant Officers can execute formal warrants; most patrol officers are simply enforcers for whoever is currently paying them.

The Inspectors

The Incorruptible Problem

A separate investigative corps appointed by the Lord Governor. Notably, Inspectors are often foreigners with no close ties within Doskvol — this is deliberate. They are designed to be outside the city's bribery network. They are famously incorruptible, which makes them uniquely dangerous and uniquely unpopular with everyone else in the enforcement system. They investigate crimes and present evidence for warrants and trials. Think of them as special prosecutors who exist specifically because the regular system cannot be trusted.

Their findings can be buried at the magistrate level — but getting to that point requires the Inspector to be wrong, and they usually aren't. The more effective approach is to keep them occupied with things that don't matter. Powerful interests are good at this.

The Magistracy

No Juries

Magistrates are appointed by the Lord Governor and prosecute trials without juries. There is no jury of peers. The magistrate decides. In practice, convictions proceed on the basis of pockets and political connections, not jurisprudence. The system is designed — whether intentionally or through accumulated tradition — to deliver justice commensurate with wealth.

"Everyone is guilty in Doskvol — make sure you get rich before you get caught."

The Spirit Wardens

Imperial Authority — Not City Government

The Spirit Wardens are a distinct and special case. They do not officially belong to Doskvol law enforcement at all — they serve the Immortal Emperor directly. This means even the Lord Governor cannot formally override them. They are a city-within-a-city operating on their own mandate, and they have been doing so long enough that everyone has simply accepted it.

Organization

Completely anonymous. Members cut all ties — no family, no close relationships outside the Wardens, code names only. Bronze masks worn at all times on duty. Based at Bellweather Crematorium for operations and the Master Warden's estate in Whitecrown for administration. Their specific mandate: respond to every death in Doskvol; destroy ghosts (not imprison — destroy, through electroplasmic furnaces); hunt rogue spirits, vampires, undead, and demons operating within city limits.

The criminal underworld will pay well to intercept a body before the deathseeker crows arrive and the Warden teams deploy. Getting there first is a time-sensitive operation measured in minutes. The Wardens are very fast.

How It Actually Works

The Real System

The wealthy have charges dropped, receive house arrest, or face exile. The poor go to Ironhook. Noble houses maintain private security — their compounds are not subject to normal Watch jurisdiction in practice. The Spirit Wardens are the one enforcement arm that actually functions regardless of social class, which is why even powerful people give them a wide berth. The Inspectors are the other genuine check — which is why influential interests work hard to keep them occupied with lesser matters.

Ironhook Prison

Located in Dunslough. Not a rehabilitation facility. A holding environment for people the city has given up on, run by Bluecoats who answer to Commander Clelland. Conditions are what you'd expect. Getting someone out requires either the right connections or enough coin to make it worth a Bluecoat officer's calculated risk. Escaping on your own is not impossible — it has been done — but the deathlands start where the lightning barrier ends.
