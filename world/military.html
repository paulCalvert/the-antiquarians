Spies, Soldiers & the State

Who commands the armies, how information moves, and what it means to have lived through the war.

Command Structure

Who Controls What

Military force in Doskvol is fragmented by design — the Imperium does not allow any single local commander to hold all the power. The land army answers to General Rowan (House Rowan). The Imperial Navy answers to Grand Admiral Dunvil (House Dunvil). The City Watch — the Bluecoats — answers to Commander Clelland (House Clelland). The Lord Governor has nominal authority over all of them, but in practice each commander answers upward to the Emperor, not sideways to the Governor's office. This creates constant jurisdictional friction that skilled operators know how to exploit.

The Garrison

Imperial Army units are stationed in Doskvol permanently — not to defend against foreign invasion, but to maintain order and back up the Watch when things escalate beyond Bluecoat capacity. The garrison is headquartered in Whitecrown, where Colonel Evelina Rowan commands the Lord Governor's stronghold. Most garrison soldiers spend their careers doing nothing; a few develop local contacts and side arrangements. Ex-garrison soldiers are one of the more common sources of dangerous, trained muscle in the underworld.

The practical line between "military matter" and "Watch matter" is wherever the person with the most influence wants it to be. If General Rowan decides something is a military affair, the Bluecoats step back. If Commander Clelland wants jurisdiction, he usually gets it below a certain level of visibility. The Lord Governor mediates — badly, and in his own interest.

The Skovlan War

The Last Free Nation

Roughly twelve years ago, the Immortal Emperor's forces completed the conquest of Skovlan — the last independent nation on the continent. The war was brutal. Skovlan had held out longer than anyone expected, fighting a grinding defensive campaign against the full weight of the Imperial military. When it ended, it ended badly for Skovland: absorption into the Empire, dissolution of their governing structures, and a peace that most Skovlans experience as ongoing occupation. The processing facilities in Lockport — the leviathan blood refineries — are staffed almost entirely by Skovlan labor under conditions that are not technically slavery but are close enough to make the distinction academic.

Veterans in the City

Both sides of the war left veterans in Doskvol. Imperial veterans with honorable discharges may have found work in the Watch, the garrison, or private security for noble houses. Those who were discharged under less favorable circumstances — disciplinary issues, injury, politics — ended up wherever former soldiers without options end up. Skovlan veterans who made it to Doskvol are a different category: they fought against the Empire, and they know it. Some of them are still fighting, quietly. The Grinders are the most visible expression of this — Skovlan workers who have not accepted what was done to them.

Having fought in the war is a specific kind of social marker in Doskvol. Imperial veterans command a certain respect from establishment figures and are viewed with suspicion by the Skovlan community. Skovlan veterans command fierce loyalty from their own and are watched carefully by the Watch and the Imperial garrison. There is no neutral position. Which side you were on follows you.

Intelligence & Information

How Information Moves

There is no single intelligence service in Doskvol — there are competing ones. The Lord Governor's office runs its own network of informants, focused on political threats to Imperial authority. Noble houses maintain private intelligence operations, usually through trusted household members, lawyers, or commercial contacts. The Ministry of Preservation has been quietly expanding its information-gathering capacity under the cover of its stated conservation mission. The criminal factions do the same — The Unseen operates almost entirely as an intelligence and influence network rather than a traditional criminal gang. Everyone is watching everyone else, and the most valuable thing in the city is reliable information no one else has.

The Inspectors

The closest thing Doskvol has to a formal investigative intelligence service. Appointed by the Lord Governor, often recruited from outside the city specifically to prevent local entanglements. They investigate crimes and build cases — but their methods require gathering information across social strata, and the best Inspectors develop networks that go far beyond any single case. They are incorruptible by design, which makes them valuable, dangerous, and deeply unpopular with everyone who profits from the city's normal arrangements. See Law & Enforcement ?

What Intelligence Work Actually Looks Like

In practice: cultivating contacts across social layers (servants who overhear conversations; dock workers who notice cargo; merchants who know when someone's finances change); maintaining dead drops and coded messages; building cover identities that can pass in multiple social contexts; trading information rather than selling it — a favor exchanged is a relationship created, not just a transaction completed. The best operators in this city are known to people across a dozen factions and owe and are owed in roughly equal measure. When they need something done, they can usually find someone who owes them.

Doskvol Academy

Where Officers Are Made

The Academy trains naval officers, garrison officers, and leviathan hunter crews. Admission is by noble birth, political appointment, or demonstrated exceptional ability — the last category exists in theory more than practice, but exceptions happen. The curriculum covers tactics, logistics, spark-craft technology, navigation, and command. Academy graduates form a tight social network of obligation and rivalry; they know each other, owe each other, and compete with each other for the rest of their careers. An Academy ring or Academy mannerisms opens certain doors immediately and closes others just as fast.

The Academy's social network extends well into the criminal upper tier. Former Academy graduates who ended up in the underworld — through debt, disgrace, or choice — still know their former classmates. A disgraced officer is a specific and useful kind of contact: well-trained, well-connected, and no longer constrained by the rules he was trained to enforce.

The Ministry of Preservation

The Bureaucratic Play

The Ministry's stated mandate is managing the city's electroplasm supply and leviathan hunting operations — environmental preservation in a world where the hunting grounds are going barren. In practice, the Ministry has been using this crisis to argue for expanded authority over the noble fleet, the refinery operations, and eventually the harbor. They are building a parallel power structure beneath the existing one: an administrative apparatus that, if completed, would give them veto authority over the most important economic activity in the city. They are not military in the traditional sense — their weapons are paper, appointments, and regulatory authority. But a Ministry official with the right stamp can stop a leviathan hunter fleet from sailing. That is power.

Angles for Characters

The Fighter's Position

A trained fighter in Doskvol has options — most of them morally complicated. Former military muscle goes to: noble house security (stable, well-paid, politically entangling); the Bluecoats (easier entry than the garrison, immediate corruption exposure); independent mercenary work (freedom, instability); or the underworld directly. The underworld actually values military training highly. Someone who can hold a position under fire, follow orders when it matters, and not lose discipline in chaos is rare and expensive. The question for any character with a military background isn't what skills they have — it's what they're willing to do with them now, and for whom.

The Tracker's Position

Hunting people — or things — in a city like Doskvol is a specific discipline. The deathlands don't allow pursuit; the city is the arena. Urban tracking means reading social patterns, not physical ones: who goes where at what time, which routes get used under pressure, how a person's behavior changes when they're being followed. A Hound in the city is part investigator, part predator, part interpreter of human habit. The city's layers — legitimate streets, canal routes, undercity passages — mean a good tracker knows which layer their target moves in, and can shift between layers faster than the target expects. The Inspectors use this skill. So do the noble intelligence networks. So does anyone trying to close a gap.

Both of these specialties — controlled violence and precise pursuit — are most interesting when they exist in tension with something. Who taught you? What did that cost? What orders have you followed that you shouldn't have, and which ones did you refuse? The military and intelligence worlds in Doskvol are full of obligations that don't end when you leave the service. Some people are still looking for former operatives. Some of those people are owed things. Some are owed debts that can only be paid one way.
