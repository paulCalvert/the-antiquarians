Lady Crane

"Hello, dearies." Tall, warm, and knows every canal in Doskvol. A genuine friend to the crew — and one of the more unusual people they've met.

Appearance

  • Of Tycherosi descent — over nine feet tall, almost entirely limbs
  • Spindly, gangly build — like a heron that learned to walk upright and never quite stopped being a heron
  • Warm and friendly despite (or because of) being deeply strange to look at
  • Due to her appearance, she works mainly at night or in the undercity canals — she fits better in the dark

Personality

  • Calls the crew "dearies" — genuinely affectionate, not condescending
  • Very friendly; the crew's most comfortable Gondoliers contact
  • Knows the waterways, the docks, and the people and cargo that move through both
  • Friend of Klyra, one of Alaric's social contacts — the connection that first brought her into the crew's orbit

Tithesday

The Harbor Superstition

Lady Crane talks about Tidesday (also called Tithesday) — the first of every month, when gondoliers, captains, dockers, and sailors toss a coin or something of value into the harbor. Not everyone does it, but most do. It's superstition: you do it to keep the harbor safe. Nobody remembers why. The name shifts between "Tidesday" and "Tithesday" depending on who's talking — the meaning has eroded but the habit hasn't. Lady Crane participates without question. It's just what you do.

What It Actually Is

Tithesday is a degraded echo of the original harbor compact obligations — the noble signatories' duty to honor the Watcher, passed down through generations until the meaning was lost and only the ritual remained. The coins mean nothing to the Watcher. The habit does.

Relationship to the Crew

  • Solid and reliable contact — not compromised, not obviously in anyone's pocket
  • Relationship established in Session 03; not yet deeply tested
  • The Gondoliers' connection to the harbor means she may become strategically important as the compact threads develop
