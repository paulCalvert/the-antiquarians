Ash-Hand — Shrine Keeper

Palm-Mother Edda

She keeps the hand-shrines. She was there before the cultists. She will be there after.

Who She Is

The Old Woman
  • An old woman found throughout the city and below it — in dock alcoves, canal junctions, building cellars, the undercity passages
  • Builds and tends tiny hand-shrines: children’s gloves stuffed with grey ash, bent coins arranged in patterns, chalk handprints pressed onto walls near the floor
  • Looks like a homeless prophet. Sounds like one too. Whether the act is genuine, performed, or something in between is unclear.
  • Fully unsettling to be near, in a way that doesn’t have an obvious source
Her Shrines

Show up in places before people have reason to look there. Her shrine appeared in the Sunken Temple’s outer hall — she was there before the Compact’s field cell arrived, and she was there when the crew arrived after that.

The Spirit Wardens remove the ones they find. They don’t find them all. The ones they miss keep getting tended.

What She Knows

More Than She Shows

She speaks in fragments and implications. Getting a straight answer from her requires either patience, the right framing, or something that actually interests her — and it’s not always obvious what that is.

She has never been threatening directly. She has never needed to be. The shrines are enough. The being-there-first is enough.

Harrow’s Training

Compact Notaries are trained to recognize and avoid disturbing her shrines. That instruction came from somewhere. Whether she is actively working for Setarra or simply drawn to her presence is not a question anyone in the Compact’s lower rings asks aloud.

GM Note

Use her as an omen and a loose thread. She surfaces before something important. Her shrines mark places of significance. She can deliver cryptic information that proves useful, or information that proves useful and wrong, or information that is right but means something different than the crew thought.

She is not reachable through normal social mechanics. She is not afraid of the crew, not interested in deals, not persuadable by logic. She is interested in what she is interested in. Find out what that is and you have a lever. Don’t, and she is simply there — unsettling, persistent, and ahead of you every time.

Connections

  • The Ash-Hand Compact — shrine keeper; her relationship to Setarra’s network is unclear from the inside
  • Harrow — knows her shrines; was trained not to disturb them; may have encountered her in person
  • The Sunken Temple — her shrine was in the outer hall before anyone else arrived
  • The City — she is throughout it, in the margins; the city knows her in the way it knows old things
