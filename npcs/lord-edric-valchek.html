House Valchek

Lord Edric Valchek

Head of House Valchek. Signs documents. Attends functions. Delegates everything else.

Who He Is

  • Nominal head of House Valchek — late sixties, polite, forgettable
  • Maintains appearances out of obligation, not ambition — knows the house is declining and has made his peace with it
  • Attends the right functions, wears the right clothes, says the right things at Council-adjacent events
  • Delegates actual management entirely to Marla Vale, who runs the house in practice

What He Knows

Not Much

Lord Edric knows his house holds old instruments and deeds. He knows they are worth less than they once were. He does not know the specific history of any individual asset — that is Marla's domain. He was aware the warehouse held "part of an old transit charter" and considers its theft a minor embarrassment that Marla is handling appropriately.

He has no vendetta. He has no resources to sustain one. If the crew were ever to interact with him directly, his response to the theft would be more focused on the reputational awkwardness than on retribution. He is more worried about embarrassment than revenge.

GM Notes

How to Use Him

Edric is not a threat. He is set dressing for House Valchek's decline and a reminder that the house's danger comes entirely from Marla, not from him. Use him in social scenes — Brightstone dinners, Council-adjacent functions — as a source of mild embarrassment and inadvertent information. He talks freely about things he doesn't understand the significance of.

He might mention the transit charter theft in conversation without realizing who he's talking to. He might describe Marla's investigation in vague, approving terms. He would absolutely describe some of the house's older instruments in a way that reveals more than he intends.
