Ash-Hand — Courier

The Handless Clerk

He delivers the notes. He does not stay.

What Is Known

The Courier
  • No name. No visible hands — sleeves are always pinned shut, or his coat is long enough that hands would be there but aren’t
  • Delivers messages, receipts, and warnings across the Compact’s cells
  • Every note he delivers smells faintly of dust and old rain
  • He is always calm. He is never in a hurry. He is never there when you look back.
  • Harrow has received messages from him. He knows him only as the Compact’s courier — nothing beyond the role.
What He Signals

His appearance means the Compact knows something. Receiving a message from him means you are being tracked, warned, or summoned — depending on the content.

He is a campaign omen device as much as a character. When he shows up, something is moving that the crew didn’t know was moving.

What He Actually Is

Theories in the Compact’s Lower Rings
  • A ghost bound to a clerk’s body, delivering messages he once failed to send in life
  • A man whose hands were taken as payment on a contract that went wrong
  • Not one person but three who share the role by rotation, each in identical coats
  • Something the Compact created, not recruited

None of these have been confirmed. None have been denied.

GM Note

Keep him ambiguous. His value is in the not-knowing. If the crew investigates, let them get close without getting there — each lead adds texture without resolution. He is most useful as the Compact’s face when the Compact wants to be noticed without being confronted.

Connections

  • The Ash-Hand Compact — courier across cells; knows the network’s structure through the messages he carries
  • Harrow — has delivered messages to him; knows him only as the courier
