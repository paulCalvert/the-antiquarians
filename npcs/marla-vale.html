House Valchek

Marla Vale

House Factor and Charter Steward. Not a Valchek by blood. The only reason the house still functions.

Who She Is

  • House Factor and Charter Steward for House Valchek — manages leases, legal instruments, city relationships, and anything Lord Edric can't be troubled to understand
  • Not a Valchek by blood — she works for them because the work suits her, not out of loyalty
  • Was present during the warehouse heist (Session 04) — survived, assessed, and has been building a case ever since
  • Sharp, practical, and quietly resentful of how much she manages for how little credit

How She Operates

Accountant, Not Soldier

Marla does not hire assassins. She files reports, leans on City Works contacts, and engages cheap muscle when necessary — the minimum force required to make a legal and administrative point. She is dangerous the way paperwork is dangerous: slowly, inexorably, and with documentation.

She noticed the theft. She filed a report. She has been cross-referencing it with everything unusual that has happened in or around Valchek holdings since. She is methodical, patient, and right more often than not.

What She Knows About the Crew

More than they'd like. Alaric's noble background made him identifiable. She has a partial description of at least two others. She doesn't know where they operate from — yet. Her investigation is slow because House Valchek's resources are limited and she won't overextend on a theft case. But she hasn't dropped it.

Pressure on the Crew

  • Alaric is specifically under Valchek suspicion — his noble background makes him the easiest thread to pull
  • The longer the crew stays at Heat 7+, the faster her clock ticks
  • She will try to leverage before she moves openly — exposure, a quiet deal, or an embarrassing report to the right people

The Deeper Thread

She Suspects the Coin Mattered

Marla has been doing this long enough to know which Valchek holdings are junk and which are weird. The token from the warehouse was in a category she had mentally filed as "probably weird, not worth digging into." She avoids digging too deep because the last time a Valchek asset turned out to be genuinely significant, the resulting complications nearly cost the house what little political standing it still had.

But she's been watching. The warehouse theft was too targeted. Someone knew what they were looking for. That level of specificity implies a source — someone who knew what the token was for. She hasn't identified the source yet. When she does, it opens a door she may or may not want to walk through.

Future Hook — The Quiet Deal

At some point Marla may decide she's more interested in what the crew knows than in prosecuting them for the theft. If she figures out the token's real significance — or if the crew's activities bring Valchek holdings into a wider conflict she can't manage alone — she becomes available for a conversation. Not friendly. Not forgiving. But practical.

"I'm not here about the warehouse. I'm here because someone in your orbit has been very interested in what House Valchek holds, and I'd like to understand why before it becomes my problem."
