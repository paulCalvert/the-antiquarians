The Wraiths — Leader

Slate

Leader of the Wraiths. Secretive, daring, sophisticated. He hasn't smiled in years — physically cannot. That doesn't mean he isn't amused.

Appearance

  • Dreadlocks
  • Paralyzed grimace — a permanent fixed expression; he cannot smile. The muscles don't work. This is unnerving in ways that are hard to articulate.
  • Broad frame — physically imposing beneath the sophistication

Personality

  • Secretive — the Wraiths' whole ethos flows from him: masks, aliases, sign language, drop sites
  • Daring — he didn't build this operation by playing it safe
  • Sophisticated — not what you expect when you hear "gang leader." He thinks several moves ahead.

The Independence Problem

Hive vs. Wraiths

The Hive wants to buy out the Wraiths — or terminate them if that fails. Slate refuses to give up the Wraiths' independence. This standoff has no easy resolution. He knows the Hive is bigger than he is. He also knows that folding means the end of what he built. The leviathan maps are the most immediate pressure point: the Wraiths can't find a buyer, the Hive is interested, and neither side can close the deal.

Connections

  • Loop — the Wraiths' appraisal expert; Slate relies on his valuations
  • Spur — newest member; Slate's assessment of her is TBD
  • The Cabbies — the Wraiths' primary ally; their information network runs through cab drivers
