The Unseen

Rook

A cabbie with a clean beard and a nice hat. An operative of The Unseen. He has never met the crew — but his faction is opposed to The Hive, and the Antiquarians are Hive-aligned.

What the Crew Knows

  • The orders directing the Crows to steal the Spirit Wardens outfit were signed simply "R"
  • The note bore the logo of a high-tier faction above the Crows in the power hierarchy
  • The crew now believes this faction is The Unseen
  • "Rook" is his handle within the organization

Who He Is

  • Operative of The Unseen — a criminal secret society operating through intermediaries
  • Appearance: cleanly maintained beard, a nice bowler hat, works as a cabbie
  • The cabbie cover is ideal — he moves through the city, sees everything, and no one looks twice
  • Has not met the Antiquarians and is not actively hunting them

The Threat

Faction Opposition

The Unseen and The Hive are opposed factions. The Antiquarians are Hive-aligned. This means Rook — and The Unseen more broadly — have structural reasons to work against the crew, even without any direct history. Rook may employ other agencies or operatives to create friction for them. He doesn't need to confront them directly to be a problem.

The Spirit Wardens Outfit

The outfit is still sitting in the Sunken City, unsold. Rook — or whoever in The Unseen directed him — wanted it badly enough to contract the Crows. The reason is still unknown. Someone with these resources and this level of secrecy has a specific purpose in mind.
