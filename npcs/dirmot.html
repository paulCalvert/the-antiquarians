Ash-Hand — Contractor

Dirmot Eckardd

Hired technical operator. Former partner of Victor's. Does careful work for whoever pays well and doesn't ask too many questions.

Who He Is

Appearance

Squat, broad, and deceptively solid — there is a layer of softness over real muscle, the build of a man who has spent years hauling heavy equipment in tight spaces. Thick neck. Wide face made wider by broad, pronounced sideburns. A worn, leathery face creased in a way that makes his age genuinely hard to place. He wears a brown bowler hat with a jimmy bar tucked horizontally through the ribbon — equal parts fashion statement and practical habit. Worn white shirt, open at the collar. Brown breeches and suspenders. Functional, not distinguished.

He carries precision instruments with the specific careful attention of someone who respects equipment he did not design. His expression at rest is neutral and watchful. He gives nothing away about what he thinks of a situation until he has decided what to do about it.

How He Operates

Dirmot is a technician, not a believer. He takes commissions, does the work, asks what he needs to ask, and no more than that. He has a pragmatist's relationship with his employers: the money matters, the mission does not, and he stays away from whatever happens after he hands off the deliverable. He has been doing work like this for a long time and has developed a reliable cost-benefit instinct for when a situation is about to become more expensive than the fee.

He will give up information to make a problem go away. He is not heroic. He is practical.

Connection to Victor

Former Partner

Dirmot and Victor worked together — the exact nature and duration of the partnership is deliberately vague. What matters: Victor knows his face, knows his methods, and knows his maker's mark. When Victor saw Dirmot's specialized drilling and boring equipment in the hands of the Ash Compact cultists at the Sunken Temple (Session 7), he recognized the tool housing. He said nothing in the moment. That thread is still open.

Whether Dirmot knows who he is ultimately supplying — a knowing collaborator, a mercenary filling commissions without asking, or something more complex — leave ambiguous. That question belongs to a future scene.

The Estate — What He Was Doing

Session 8 — The Dalmorrian Sub-Level

Dirmot was hired to take specific readings of the pale roads beneath the Dalmorrian estate and hand the data to an Ash Compact contact the following day. He was told what to measure, not why. He did not ask. The money was good.

The Dalmorrian house sits on a convergence point where spiritual current channels run together, anchored by the bloodline's compact obligations. Setarra wants to know what happens to this convergence when the compact transfers to her — she is mapping what comes after, not just what happens now. Dirmot is one instrument in that survey. He does not know the larger picture.

What He Knows / Doesn't Know

Knows: He is measuring spiritual current beneath the house. It is Ash Compact work. He will hand off data tomorrow. The money is good.

Does not know: Why the measurements matter. Who ultimately commissioned the work. That the compact is being transferred to Setarra. That anything is happening upstairs tonight.

If the Survey Was Interrupted

If the crew took or destroyed his equipment, the measurements are incomplete. Setarra is missing data about this specific convergence node. The crew will not know they won this — not tonight. But she will be working without a piece of her map.
