The Conductor

What the Crew Saw

A spectral figure in a conductor's uniform — old style, pre-Cataclysm livery. He moved through the train with authority, as though the 5th Line was still his. He didn't attack. He was watching. When the crew got what they came for, he was gone — not dispersed, not destroyed. Just no longer there.

He gave the impression of someone with a purpose that the crew had not yet crossed. The fact that he didn't try to stop them from taking the Reliquary was either reassuring or should have been more unsettling than it was.

What He Actually Is

Not a ghost in the conventional sense. The Conductor is the city's most deliberate expression — something Doskvol crystallized around a specific function when the compact started being pulled toward Setarra. His purpose: keep the compact intact, resist what Scurlock is doing, stop what is being set in motion.

He couldn't stop the crew from taking the Reliquary — that would have required direct confrontation with Scurlock's plan, and Scurlock is too old and too woven into the city's fabric to oppose directly. What the Conductor can do is work sideways: nudge people toward each other, engineer meetings, put the right person in motion at the right time. Wren didn't find the crew by accident.

He vanished from the train because the score ending wasn't his moment. He is not done. He is patient in the way a city is patient — everything in the long run, everything eventually.

After the Train

Setarra's Visit

The crew returned to the Sunken City with the Reliquary and were met by Setarra herself. She was ominous and direct: she knows they're involved, she wants the Reliquary, and Scurlock cannot protect them.

Scurlock's Revelation

The crew delivered the Reliquary to Scurlock. He confirmed it's only part of a larger ritual — two more items are needed before anything can be done with it. He has leads.
