Session 07

The Sunken Temple

A puddle you should not whistle near. A door that opened when Alaric touched it. The coin, retrieved.

  • Absent: Victor, Costya (downtime resolved narratively)

The Jared Intervention

Alaric went to the warehouse to warn Jared about the Meridian Circle. Jared was in high spirits and completely unbothered. When Alaric grabbed him by the shoulders and swore at him with real intensity — very out of character — Jared was briefly startled, then laughed, gave him finger guns, and agreed to meet some friends.

What followed was a walk through the Docks, Brightstone, and Charterhall, with Jared treating every alley and lamppost like a heist. He went quieter when they entered 6 Towers. He had been there before. Harrow stepped from the shadows in his Spirit mask — and Jared immediately recognized him from a recent play and told him he was fantastic. Harrow almost forgot what they were doing.

The crew had not planned to like Jared. They came to like Jared. He tried to bolt at one point and ran straight into Brick. “Is your name… Slab?” He turned the whole intervention back onto Alaric — “Wait, you saw a demon? What are YOU into right now?” — and for a moment, the crew agreed with him.

Then Harrow put his hand on Jared and showed him what Setarra actually looks like. Jared convulsed, vomited, and fell unconscious. Alaric held him until he came back. Jared was shaken but not done. He told Alaric warmly, firmly: “I am your senior. I can make my own decisions. Doesn’t matter how many mistakes I make, I always turn out okay.” Then: “I am seriously reconsidering my relationship with these people. You have given me a lot to think about.” They hugged. Alaric walked him home.

The Score

Getting In

Brick asked Sawtooth about a hidden temple. The answer: “You know that black puddle? They say you should not whistle on black water.” Harrow’s leviathan contacts placed it under the Docks. The crew went to the Cold Corner.

The Room of Shrines

A working space — parishioners present, offerings on the altars. The crew moved through trying not to draw attention. A beggar entered to offer a piece of moldy bread. Alaric gave him a gold coin instead: “Don’t offer your last food. Offer mine.” The beggar bolted. The room noticed the strangers. Brick offered a coin at the central shrine. Alaric offered two. Brick looked at him. Alaric realized he did not know how this worked. Brick said only: “Yeah, the water is weird today.”

The Black Puddle

A collapsed alcove. Two to four inches of still, black water. The crew stood around it for a long time. Flint got tired of waiting, walked in, and whistled. He began to sink. Brick tried to pull him out and couldn’t. Flint was panicking by the time he reached his waist. Harrow walked in beside him and whistled too.

Flint went under first. The bottom was not there. Through desperate rolls he found the other side. The rest followed one by one — Alaric last, after realizing the parishioners watching the rich man alone were the worse option. Underwater, Brick grappled the wall and dragged the group forward. Alaric grabbed onto his neck a little too hard. Brick got everyone through — and glimpsed something in the deep beneath them that he did not have words for. Everyone surfaced in the next puddle, coughing.

The Monitor

The starfish-cephalopod entity was waiting. The crew looked at it; it looked back. Alaric asked for a rock. Flint offered his — the river rock his father had given him, from a far-off freshwater stream. Alaric threw it past the Monitor to test for a reaction. None. The crew gave him considerable hell. Embarrassed, Alaric walked under the Monitor to retrieve it — forgetting the Monitor was there — then remembered, froze, and the Monitor still did not move. The rest of the party walked through.

The Echo

A ruptured pump, locked in place and still running. Flint recognized the pattern: common labor practice. You lock the device while it runs so no one stops it for a break. The machine works; you work. Alaric found this explanation reasonable. That caused friction.

The Cultists and the Door

Two Ash Compact members were camped at the sealed door with a drill and a pick. Alaric talked them down — then they saw him and got very excited: “YOU can open the door!” They seized him and dragged him toward it. Something in Alaric reacted as he got close, and he froze.

Brick put a sledgehammer through one of them. The other, Harrow held. Alaric composed himself and asked what was beyond the door. The cultist didn’t know — he was whimpering about his friend. Alaric, intending to bluff: “Okay, kill him.” Harrow snapped his neck. The crew argued about this. Then the death bells started.

Alaric approached the door. He saw two unfamiliar crests and one that looked like an older, fuller version of his family’s crest — more detailed, from a time when the people who drew it had seen a real tree. He touched it. The door slid open without effort.

The Sanctum

A utilitarian room of extraordinary construction. Three ancient diving suits hung on hooks — Harrow recognized them as predecessors to the gear leviathan sailors use over the side of a ship, but much older and far more sophisticated. Brick looked into a cracked visor and saw a vision of a diver falling, falling, swallowed by darkness. The Monitor squeezed through an impossibly narrow gap in the tile and was gone.

The Sea Room and the Coin

Ankle-deep water. Barnacle-covered walls. A membrane of open seawater on the far end, and beyond it, a stone tower with something glinting at its top. The Monitor was at the base of the tower, watching. Brick did not want to go in. Flint did not want to get back in the water.

Alaric and Harrow put on two of the suits — and found, on a mixed success, that they could not get back out. Victor will probably be able to help. Harrow walked to the membrane and reached through. The coin came free without resistance. The water started rising.

Alarms. A door sealed the room. Alaric was paralyzed by the roll. On the other side, Flint read the lock — Tycherosi-style, a phrase in Hadrathi: Voice Command. Brick said: “Open?” The door opened. He told Alaric to move. Alaric moved. Brick said: “Close.” The water stopped. The Monitor watched from beyond the membrane and did not follow.

The Exit

The crew took the third diving suit, a stool of unusual quality, and some old tools. The sealed door slid closed behind them. They sent the two dead cultists through the puddle ahead of them. On the other side: two more bodies already floating down — shrine-goers who had tried to follow when they saw people sink after a whistle. The shrine room was empty. When the diving helmets broke the surface, the last few onlookers ran.

Lady Crane met them on the water and got them most of the way home. They arrived at the Sunken City, still in the suits.

GM — Score Notes

Alaric at the door: The Dalmorrian bloodline opened it. He has no framework for what this means yet — but Scurlock does. This is why Scurlock needed him from the start.

Brick’s vision: The diver in the visor is the Watcher before the Cataclysm. The pull Brick felt toward the water is the Watcher’s attention sharpening. Clock started: Beyond Takes Interest.

The coin: The Watcher did not resist. The coin is still in the city. He is watching to see what happens to it.

Heat: ~3–4. Four dead bodies, witnessed by shrine attendees, crew extracted in conspicuous ancient gear.

The suits: Alaric and Harrow are stuck. Victor gets them out next session. Decide before Session 8 whether the suits have additional properties beyond being impressive artifacts.
