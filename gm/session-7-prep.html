Session 7 — The Sunken Temple

Extended downtime, then the two-act score beneath the docks. Low stakes, high mood. Eight spaces. Character moments for Brick, Flint, Victor, Alaric.

Before You Start

Confirm Before the Session
  • House Dalmorrian crest (confirmed): A tree — pale brown trunk, yellowish-green leaves. The modern version is simplified; older versions had more detail and more leaves. The house predates the Cataclysm, so the crest was designed when trees were a living thing in the world. The people who simplified it over the generations didn't have a living reference anymore. Use this.
  • Castia: Not present this session.
  • Party state going in: Everyone at Level 1 harm, near-max stress.

Downtime — 3 Actions Each

Why 3 Actions

The crew delivered the Reliquary to Scurlock. In their minds, it's safe. They've earned breathing room. Last session was back-to-back scores with no downtime — this is the recovery they were owed. Run it efficiently: each player announces their 3 actions, resolve quickly, move on. The third action is where character texture lives.

Actions 1 & 2 — Expected for Everyone
  • Vice — dump stress. Run the vice roll, note overindulgence if it comes up.
  • Recover — Level 1 harm is a 4-segment clock. One decent Recover roll fills it. Most characters clear their harm this downtime.
Action 3 — Per Character
  • Brick — Dockworker contacts, old rumors. He finds the canal route in, but the more valuable thing he comes back with is specific: there is a puddle in the back of the shrine room that has been there as long as anyone can remember. Dockworkers leave offerings near it and don't stand in it — nobody can explain the rule, they just keep it. He heard from someone who heard from someone that if you stand in it long enough, it takes you through to the other side. He does not know exactly what that means. He brings it anyway.
  • Flint — Gather information on the underground ruins. He wants to know the structure before he trusts it. On a good roll: reward him with a detail — there's an unusual mechanism on one of the interior doors, old Akorosi, pre-standard. He'll recognize it when he sees it.
  • Victor — Studies the Hull parts recovered from the ghost train. On a good roll: the fabrication technique is older than anything he's worked with, and feels familiar in a way he can't place. Seed only — it pays off inside the temple.
  • Alaric — Something social. A contact, a letter, a parlor encounter. Someone with old money treats the Dalmorrian name with unusual weight — deference they don't explain. Plant the feeling that the name means something he doesn't understand yet.
  • Harrow — Vice and recover. He's had his spotlight. Let him rest.

Scurlock's Briefing

Delivered via Jeeves

Two more items needed beyond the Reliquary. First: a relic from the entity that holds the bay compact — something from before it became what it is. Jeeves points them toward the old docking quarter, the Undercity, a temple that predates the lightning barrier. He is precise about what he knows and careful about what he doesn't. He does not mention the six house seals or the bloodline door.

What Scurlock Actually Knows

He knows the artifact is a relic from the Watcher's human life. He knows the inner sanctum is bloodline-warded and cannot be entered without compact bloodline. He has been directing Ash Compact contacts toward it for some time — all have failed. He is directing the crew because Alaric is a Dalmorrian and the door will open for him. He has not told Alaric this. He is using him.

Engagement Roll

Setup

Brick leads them in through the canal system — a passage found during downtime. Good approach, low alarm, no known opposition. Base pool: 1d. Add for Brick's dockworker ties (+1d) and solid reconnaissance (+1d). Expect a 4–6 result. If 1–3: the canal passage is partially collapsed — costs time and resources to push through, opens the session already slightly strained.

The Score — Eight Spaces

Space 1 — The Canal Mouth

Brick leads them through a canal passage to a submerged archway — old construction, pre-barrier, easy to miss if you don't know it's there. He knows it's there.

The first wrong reflection: Brick sees it in the canal surface before they go under. A glimpse of something deeper than the water should be. He's the only one who catches it. Even here, the water feels slightly heavier than it should — not wrong enough to name, just present.

Challenge: None. This is the threshold.

Hooks: Brick as guide. The group enters on his knowledge.

Space 2 — The Outer Hall

Large vaulted space. Dockworkers have been coming here for generations without knowing why — leaving coins, rope, fish bones, crude carved figures near the walls. Some offerings are recent. Nobody told them to do this. They just felt like they should.

Harrow feels the ghost field immediately. Old deaths, old grief, nothing hostile. Just thick with age and weight.

The city-spirit seed: Central figure in the room — full standing figure, arms raised overhead, palms facing upward, fingertips pointing outward away from the body. A presenting gesture: we hold this up to you, this is yours. The dockworkers' offerings are clustered at its base. The room is organized around it. Face worn featureless by age and water.

If Harrow reads the ghost field here on the way in: honor and supplication. The city maintaining a relationship it doesn't consciously know it has. Old, settled, sincere.

On the retreat — after the coin is taken — the figure's arms are still raised, palms still upward. The fingertips now point backward, toward the body, toward where a face should be. The change is small enough to miss if they're moving. When anyone looks directly at it, both positions are visible simultaneously — a shimmer between the two, neither fully resolving.

If Harrow reads the ghost field now: fear and the unknown. The city doesn't have language for what just changed. It only knows something did.

Don't name it. Don't explain it. They took a coin and a statue's fingertips moved an inch and the ghost field went from reverence to dread.

The puddle: Against the far wall, just below the largest cluster of offerings. Six inches across at most, sitting in a shallow depression in the flagstone. Dark, still water. Coins have been tossed into it and left at the edge. Some of the offerings have been placed as close to it as possible without touching it. Nobody stands in it. It has looked like this for longer than anyone has been alive.

If anyone looks directly into it: it doesn't reflect the room. It reflects the open bay.

Brick tells them what he knows. That's everything he knows.

The commit: stepping in is not a roll. It is a choice. The first person who goes — describe their feet going through what looks like six inches of standing water and not stopping. Shin, knee, hip. Gone. The people still in the room watch. That's all they can do until it's their turn.

Challenge: None yet. Exploration, tone, and the commit moment.

Hooks: Harrow reads the field. Anyone studying the shrines learns dockworkers have been doing this for a very long time without anyone telling them to. The puddle is the door.

Space 3 — The Open Bay

The puddle takes them through. Below: nothing. The floor of the bay is so far down it isn't visible. Just open dark water going deeper than any of them can process, and the Watcher's presence making every cubic foot of it heavier than it should be. The water is thick here — the gravity of this place is the deep, not the surface. The natural motion is down.

The collapsed wall is visible above and ahead. There is a gap in it. That is the way through. It is not far. It is entirely possible to make it. The water is working against that.

Challenge: Resolve, risky. The physical distance is manageable. The problem is looking down.

  • 6: Through. They kept their eyes on the wall and swam up and they are through. Exhausted. Intact.
  • 4–5: Through, but they looked down. They made it, they're functional, but whatever they saw in the dark below them is sitting somewhere behind their eyes. 1 stress. They don't describe it to the others.
  • 1–3: They started going down instead of up. Someone already through has to come back for them — which means going back into it. Or: they made it, but they saw something. Not the Observer. Something vast and still and patient, far below, looking up. They make it through the wall and don't speak for a moment after surfacing.

Brick goes first. He's the one who knew about the puddle, he goes first. His wrong-water sense is at full volume — he knows exactly how deep the bay goes from here. He surfaces on the other side and waits.

Harrow should not look down. He looks down.

Flint — there is no mechanism here, no system to read, no problem he can solve with his hands. Just open nothing and the need to swim. That's unusual for him.

Hooks: Anyone who rolls 1–3 carries something out of the water with them. The void isn't hostile — it just is. That's worse.

Space 4 — The Watch Chamber

A larger vaulted room where several passages meet. Some side passages have partially collapsed. Old tool marks on the walls — someone was excavating here a long time ago and stopped. Pre-Cataclysm construction is visible in the ceiling vaulting. Victor notices the technique — it's unusual, older than the standard.

Challenge 1 — The Observer arrives: It drops from the ceiling without warning and lands between them and the forward passage. Starfish-cephalopod, ink-dark, arms that move wrong. One enormous eye. It does not advance. It watches. It leaves an impression rather than words: you are being catalogued.

  • Attune (Harrow) to read it — on success, he understands it's a monitor, not a predator. It reports to something vast. It is filing them away.
  • Resolve (everyone else) to hold steady. Failure: a flinch, a noise, a drawn weapon — it flows instantly into a crack and watches from inside the stone. More agitated for the rest of the score.
  • If attacked: gone before the blow lands. It cannot be fought.

Challenge 2 — Structural instability: One passage forward is actively shifting — water pressure from above, old stone giving way. Wreck or Tinker to shore it up; Prowl to slip through before it closes. Controlled if the Observer is calm. Risky if it's agitated.

Hooks: Harrow anchors the Observer. Victor notices the construction technique. Everyone is being watched and knows it.

Space 5 — The Pump Corridor

A descending passage between the Watch Chamber and the door beyond. Colder here. The air is heavier, wetter. Against one wall: the remains of a mechanical drainage pump — hand-operated, rope-and-pulley system still bolted into the stone. It ruptured. Not from age: from overpressure, from someone pushing it too hard trying to clear the flooded passages ahead. Brown staining on the stone around it. Blood, long dried.

Someone died here trying to get through to the door.

The ghost echo: Harrow sees it, or anyone who looks closely. A residual impression worn into the stone — a figure in the last moments of their life, not trying to escape, not calling for help. Trying to work a mechanism. Hands moving over the place where the pump housing was, working a lock or a valve that no longer exists. The explosion destroyed it. The echo doesn't know that. It is still trying to fix the problem, still working the phantom mechanism, over and over.

For everyone else: strange, sad, unsettling. Good set dressing.

For Flint: the specific shape of it — someone trying to undo damage that can't be undone, working a system past the point where the system can help — lands somewhere personal. He probably doesn't say anything in the moment. He probably brings it up later. Let him.

Challenge: None. This is breathing room and character texture. Move through it at whatever pace the table sets.

Hooks: Flint's resonance. Harrow reads the echo. The blood stain is a reminder that others have tried this and paid for it.

Space 6 — The Door Chamber

A wide chamber at the end of the descent. The back wall has a sealed door — heavy, pre-Cataclysm, no visible mechanism. Six house seals carved into the arch above it. Three intact. Three cracked through.

In front of it: broken picks, cracked hand drills, fresh gouges alongside old ones. And currently — one or two Ash Compact cultists camped here. Bedroll, cold food, research notes spread on the floor. Cipher marks on their gear.

Challenge — The cultists:

  • Prowl — slip past before being spotted. Hard with a group.
  • Sway / Consort — talk them down. They're territorial and paranoid but not fighters. Days of failure have worn them thin.
  • Command — make them stand down. Fast. Creates an enemy.
  • Skirmish — loud, messy, disturbs the Observer.

Before the cultist encounter resolves: Alaric sees the arch above the door. Six seals carved into the stone — and one of them is a tree. More detailed than his crest, more leaves, the trunk textured and full, but unmistakably the same symbol he's had on documents and signet rings his whole life. He's been looking at a simplified echo of this. The people who simplified it over the generations didn't have a living reference anymore. He's looking at what his family was actually remembering.

Whatever happens with the cultists, Alaric opens the door with them present. He touches the stone — nothing. Then blood: thumb against the sharp edge of the carving. The door opens. Silently. As though it was never closed. Play that beat slowly. The cultists see it happen after everything they tried. If any are still present: they react — lunge, freeze, beg. The crew gets through. The door closes. The cultists cannot follow.

Space 6 — Cultist GM Notes

Not fighters — researchers and fanatics who've been here for days hitting a wall. They don't know the crew. They don't know Scurlock's name (independent cells — "let not one hand know the work of the other"). They have begun to suspect they were given a task they were never equipped to complete.

What they carry: Research notes on the bloodline ward — partial understanding, they know it requires something inherent not mechanical. Ash Compact cipher marks on their gear. This is the first hint the crew may recognize that Setarra has an organization, not just agents.

Victor's moment — Dirmot's tools: The specialized drilling and boring equipment the cultists brought — the precision tool housings, the drive mechanisms — bear a maker's mark. Victor recognizes it when he sees the tools up close: Dirmot's workshop. His betrayer has been supplying equipment to Setarra's people. He says nothing. He looks at the mark for a moment and keeps moving. His player will do the rest.

Leave it ambiguous whether Dirmot knows who he's supplying — a knowing collaborator, a mercenary filling commissions, or something deeper. That question is a thread for later.

On the flood: When water starts seeping back through from Space 8, the cultists run. You won't need to manage them.

The Sanctum — Two Rooms

Space 7 — The Equipment Chamber

The first room beyond the bloodline door. Relatively dry — damp stone and old seepage, no standing water. No human has been here since before the Cataclysm. Different air. A quality of silence the outer temple doesn't have.

The Observer follows through the closing door impossibly — flows through the gap like something that doesn't fully obey space — and settles high on the wall. Eye open. It will not leave.

At the far end: a heavy stone door, closed, no lock — just centuries of disuse and settled weight. Flint reads the seam and the mechanics in about thirty seconds and gets it open. Clean, quiet, efficient. That's who he is. What was set-up in Space 5 pays off here — he just does the thing, no drama needed.

What's here:

  • Victor's moment — The diving apparatus. Full pre-Cataclysm suit mounted on the wall. He reads it immediately: the pressurization systems, the material seams, the joint construction. Then: the fabrication technique echoes the Hull parts from the train. Not identical. Ancestral. Like whoever built the first Hulls was working from a template no one remembered came from here. He doesn't say it out loud. He files it.
  • Brick's moment — The helmet. Mounted with the suit. He looks into the faceplate. Not his reflection — the bay, clear and alive, lit from above as though the sun still exists. A figure in a diving suit descending into it, growing smaller. The last dive. The man before he became the thing. Hold this beat. Don't rush it.
  • Alaric's moment — The six seals. Carved at the base of each wall, one per face of the hexagonal chamber. Three intact, three cracked. The Dalmorrian seal here is the most complete version he will ever see — a full tree, branches spread wide, leaves individually carved, the trunk detailed and textured. His modern crest is a stripped-down echo of this, simplified by generations who no longer had a living tree to reference. In Doskvol, trees are mythological. His family has been carrying a memory of the world before the sun went out, and here is what they were actually remembering. The stone is warm to his touch. He opened this room with his blood and the room knows it. He has no answers. He has a feeling he cannot dismiss.

Challenge: None. Discovery and character texture. Don't rush. When they're ready, Flint's door leads to the bay chamber.

Space 8 — The Bay Chamber

More water here — ankle to shin deep across much of the floor. But it doesn't move like ankle-deep water. The Watcher's presence saturates it. Every step is the resistance of waist-deep water. Cold. Heavy. The room is working against them just by existing.

At the far edge: the open bay. The water simply stops — held back by nothing visible, a vertical wall of dark seawater from floor to ceiling. Beyond it, the bay goes down into black nothing. No barrier, no glass, no mechanism. The Watcher's will, holding the ocean back.

Just beyond the membrane, sitting on a pedestal rising from the sea floor: the coin. Humble stone base, moss-covered, looks like it grew there rather than was placed. Close enough to reach. Bone dry.

The Observer is in the water. It was always in the water. That eye, just behind the membrane, watching.

Challenge — Crossing the floor: The viscous water. No roll unless someone rushes or panics — but describe the effort. Every step deliberate. Brick feels it most: the Watcher's presence is immediate here, and his wrong-water sensitivity is at its peak. Something enormous just registered their footsteps.

Taking the coin: No roll. Someone reaches through the membrane — it doesn't resist, but it doesn't feel like water. Cold beyond cold. A threshold. The coin crosses back into the room. Dry.

The membrane fails. The bay comes in. Not a wave — just water, rising from the far wall, steady and patient. And it is thick. Bay water meeting Watcher-heavy room water. Moving through it gets harder by the moment.

Space 8 — The Flood (Resistance Track, Not a Clock)

Don't count segments. Track it as a feeling that builds. Describe the water level and the resistance getting worse as they linger.

  • Early — manageable: Ankle-deep but heavy. Moving is like wading through wet sand. They can grab items, make decisions, move with purpose.
  • Mid — difficult: Shin-deep now and each step is a fight. Someone who stumbles here has a problem. Grabbing something from Space 7 is a real cost — they have to push back through the door against the resistance.
  • Late — dangerous: Anyone still in the room is struggling to stay upright. Moving more than a few feet requires a roll. The water is winning.
  • Full — impossible: They cannot push through it anymore. Anyone still in Space 8 cannot get out without desperate action.

The retreat: push back through Flint's door from Space 8 into Space 7. Flint pulls it shut. Stone on stone. The water presses against it — and holds. The bay stays in Space 8.

Once through the door, the water is normal again. The pressure is gone. They're in Space 7 and can take a breath before moving out through the rest of the temple.

The Observer stays in the water as they retreat. Perfectly still in the thickening bay water, at home in everything that's trying to hold the crew back. That eye, watching them go. The last image of the sanctum as the door closes.

Items: Anyone who wants to grab something from Space 7 (Victor and a piece of the diving apparatus, anyone else, one item) must do so against the clock of rising resistance. Let them make the choice.

The End Beat

Surfacing

They come up into the harbor district. Cold air. The city is still there. The coin is in someone's pocket, dry and unremarkable. Nothing dramatic has happened yet.

Then Brick feels it — the water beneath the docks shifts. Not a wave. Not a current. Something vast and old, patient as stone, just turned its head and looked directly at them for the first time.

The Watcher knows.

End the session there.

Session Notes

Threads — Leave Open, Don't Address
  • The Conductor — loose end, no plans. Don't touch it this session.
  • The second item — Scurlock reveals it next session. Involves the noble house side of the compact. The Dalmorrian thread deepens from there.
  • Marla Vale's investigation clock — ticking toward Alaric. Don't trigger it this session.
  • Victor's fabrication discovery — planted in Space 7, not explained. Let it sit alongside the Dirmot tools moment from Space 6.
  • Dirmot and the Ash Compact — leave ambiguous. Knowing collaborator, mercenary contractor, or something deeper — that question belongs to a future session.
  • Ash Compact as an organization — the cultists suggest structure behind Setarra. Don't confirm it explicitly. Let players wonder.
The City Spirit — One Seed Only

The central statue in Space 2. Describe the pose going in — arms overhead, palms up, fingertips pointing outward. On the retreat, call it back: same pose, same palms, fingertips now pointing backward. Both positions visible simultaneously when looked at directly. Don't name it, don't explain it.

The ghost field reading is the interpretive layer: honor and supplication going in, fear and the unknown coming out. The city has been tending the compact through the dockworkers' folk behavior — the shrines, the Tide Tithe, the puddle nobody stands in. It doesn't know what the crew just took. It knows something changed.

This is a seed. The city-spirit has no further role this session.

The Watcher — Background Context

Pre-Cataclysm bay diver. Superstitious — tossed a coin before every dive. Was submerged when the Cataclysm hit; a deep demon merged with him. Still bound to protect the bay. The Tide Tithe is an echo of his personal ritual. The coin is his last toss — the one he never made. He makes his water thick and heavy — the closer to his domain, the more his presence is felt in the water itself. He is not yet hostile to the crew. After tonight, he is interested.
