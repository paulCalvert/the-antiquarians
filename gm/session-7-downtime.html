Session 07

Downtime & Approach

Three actions each, Scurlock's briefing, the engagement roll, and what's moving in the background before the score.

Before You Start

Table Check
  • Castia: Not present this session.
  • Party state going in: Everyone at Level 1 harm, near-max stress.
  • House Dalmorrian crest (confirmed with Andrew): A tree — pale brown trunk, yellowish-green leaves. Modern version is simplified. The house predates the Cataclysm; older versions had more detail and leaves. The people who simplified it over generations no longer had a living tree to reference. Use this in Spaces 6 and 7.

Downtime — 3 Actions Each

Why 3 Actions

Back-to-back scores with no downtime — this is what they were owed. Run it efficiently. Each player announces their 3 actions, resolve quickly, move on. The third action is where the texture lives.

Actions 1 & 2 — Everyone
  • Vice — dump stress, note overindulgence
  • Recover — Level 1 harm clears on one decent roll. Most characters enter the score clean.
Action 3 — Per Character
  • Brick — Dockworker contacts, old rumors. Finds the canal route in, but the more valuable thing: there's a puddle in the back of the shrine room that's been there as long as anyone remembers. Dockworkers don't stand in it — nobody can explain the rule, they just keep it. If you stand in it long enough, it takes you through to the other side. He doesn't know exactly what that means.
  • Flint — Gather info on the ruins. On a good roll: there's an unusual mechanism on one of the interior doors, old Akorosi, pre-standard. He'll recognize it when he sees it.
  • Victor — Studies the Hull parts from the ghost train. On a good roll: the fabrication technique is older than anything he's worked with and feels familiar in a way he can't place. Seed only — pays off in Space 7.
  • Alaric — Something social. Someone with old money treats the Dalmorrian name with unusual deference they don't explain. Plant the feeling that the name means something he doesn't understand yet. See the Jared section below — this is where he first hears about the society.
  • Harrow — Vice and recover only. Let him rest.

The Jared Clock

Clock — "Jared Commits" (6 segments) — Start at 2

Setarra has been patient with Jared. The crew taking the coin changes her timeline. She accelerates. The clock starts ticking this downtime — it is already at 2 by the time the session begins, meaning Jared has already attended initial gatherings and is enthusiastic. The crew does not know this yet.

  • Ticks 1–2 (already filled): Jared attends initial gatherings of the society. Finds the people remarkable. Feels special for having been noticed.
  • Tick 3: He's asked to bring a family document to the next meeting — something with the Dalmorrian seal on it. He sees nothing unusual in this. He goes home and looks through his correspondence.
  • Tick 4: He brings the document. The society leader examines it with unusual interest. Jared finds this flattering. He's invited to a more private inner gathering.
  • Tick 5: He attends the inner gathering. It involves a ritual component he half-understands. He feels something shift but can't name it. He doesn't tell Alaric.
  • Tick 6: The ritual transfer is ready. A living Dalmorrian signatory, willing, present, with a family relic. Everything Setarra needs.
How Alaric Hears About It (Downtime Seed)

During Alaric's downtime social action — at a gathering, in the middle of the unusual deference to his name — someone mentions almost in passing: "Your uncle has been in good spirits lately. He's been attending the Meridian Circle's gatherings — some new society, quite fashionable in certain quarters. Do you know it?"

They say it lightly, as though it's ordinary gossip. It might be. The name lands and the conversation moves on. Alaric is left deciding whether to pursue it.

The Meridian Circle — What It Actually Is

An Ash Compact front operation, active for about six months. It presents as a scholarly society for minor nobles with historical interests — old bloodlines, pre-Cataclysm lore, suppressed knowledge about the city's founding families. Everything Jared would find irresistible.

Membership is selective, flattering, and by invitation only. The invitation to Jared was not random — Setarra's network has been mapping the compact bloodlines for years. He was identified as the most accessible Dalmorrian: well-meaning, lonely, susceptible to prestige, no close enough family who'd ask uncomfortable questions. Except Alaric.

The society meets in rented parlor space in Brightstone. The leader is Veleris Quill — presents as a scholar of noble genealogy, enthusiastic, knowledgeable, warm. Always gloved. Speaks as though everything is already documented. Jared finds her impressive and oddly reassuring — she treats his bloodline as though it has legal weight, because in her framework it does. One or two other members Alaric might recognize as connected to past Ash Compact activity if he thinks back.

If Alaric Pursues — Investigation Options
  • Gather Information: Learn what the Meridian Circle actually is. On a strong roll: he identifies an Ash Compact cipher mark in their materials or recognizes a member from a prior encounter. The connection to Setarra becomes clear or strongly implied.
  • Talk to Jared directly: See below. Hard to navigate without revealing too much.
  • Investigate the society: Attend a gathering under a pretext, identify the leadership, find the connection. Costs a downtime action and possibly creates exposure — the society will notice a Dalmorrian showing up uninvited.
  • Do nothing: The clock ticks. Jared attends more meetings. The window to intervene cheaply closes.
If Alaric Warns Jared — How to Navigate It

Jared will not believe vague warnings. He's been attending for weeks, he finds the people wonderful, and Alaric has a pattern (from Jared's perspective) of being paranoid about occult matters ever since "that unpleasant business" with Setarra. He'll dismiss it gently. "I'm sure you're being cautious, Alaric, but these are perfectly respectable people."

What Jared responds to:

  • Specific evidence about specific members — if Alaric can name someone in the society as dangerous with a credible reason, Jared pauses. He's not stupid, just trusting. Concrete information moves him.
  • A counter-offer — Jared joined the society because it made him feel special and historically significant. If Alaric gives him something that satisfies that need — telling him about the Dalmorrian seal in the temple, what it means, that the family is part of something old and important — Jared might lose interest in the society. But this reveals what Alaric knows. Which opens questions about how he knows it. Which opens everything else.
  • Scurlock as a resource — Alaric could ask Scurlock to deal with the society. Scurlock would be interested to hear about it — Ash Compact cells operating on independent agendas are a complication for him too. But bringing this to Scurlock means telling him the crew knows about it. Another card played.

The core tension: protecting Jared requires revealing information. Every option that works has a cost. Let Alaric's player sit with that.

Scurlock's Briefing

Delivered via Jeeves — Before the Score

Two more items needed beyond the Reliquary. First: a relic from the entity that holds the bay compact — something from before it became what it is. Jeeves points them toward the old docking quarter, the Undercity, a temple that predates the lightning barrier. Precise about what he knows, careful about what he doesn't. Does not mention the six house seals or the bloodline door.

What Scurlock Actually Knows

He knows the artifact is a relic from the Watcher's human life. He knows the inner sanctum is bloodline-warded. All his Ash Compact contacts have failed to enter it. He is directing the crew because Alaric is a Dalmorrian and the door will open for him. He has not told Alaric this. He is using him.

After the Score — The Handoff

The crew surfaces with the coin. At some point Scurlock will want it. How this plays out depends on what the crew does. This scene happens after the score, but prep it now — it is the bridge to the next act of the campaign.

Running Visual — The Dust

Throughout this scene and every Scurlock appearance going forward: dust settles on his cape. He brushes it away mid-sentence, without comment, without pausing the conversation. Do not explain it. At the ritual scene, he unclasps the cape and lets it fall — a grey billow on impact. He doesn’t pick it up.

Scurlock's Move — Jeeves Arrives

Within a day or two: Jeeves arrives at the Sunken City with a polite inquiry. Lord Scurlock is eager to secure the artifact at the earliest convenience. He phrases it as consideration for their safety — "safer with him than in the field, given the current attention the crew has attracted." The subtext is not subtle.

If they delay, Jeeves comes again. Still polite. Slightly less warm. A third time is not a request.

Option A — They Hand It Over Promptly

Scurlock receives it in person or through Jeeves. He examines it briefly — just a moment, then sets it aside. Without looking down, he brushes dust from his shoulder — quick, habitual, and gone before most people would register it. Perceptive crew members catch something: not gratitude. Relief. Like a man who has been holding his breath for a very long time.

He informs them of the third item: "One component remains. The compact requires a relic representing the mortal signatories — the human side of the original obligation. Something from one of the six bloodlines, carried forward to the present day." He pauses. Looks at Alaric. "The Dalmorrian house may hold what we need. I'll look into what form it takes and be in touch."

He doesn't say what specifically. He doesn't explain what he plans to do with all three components. He thanks them and sees them out. As they leave, he is already brushing dust from his sleeve again. He doesn't notice himself doing it.

The implication lands in the carriage ride home, if not in the room: he has been pointing Alaric at his own family's history this entire time. The Dalmorrian name opened the door. The Dalmorrian blood is the third item. He needed Alaric from the start.

Option B — They Delay

The delay is itself information. Scurlock notes it. He doesn't escalate immediately — he is patient — but he begins paying closer attention to what the crew is doing and saying.

The upside: the crew has time with the coin. Harrow can attempt a Long Read ritual on it — the object carries the weight of the Watcher's pre-Cataclysm life, his last dive, the moment of the Cataclysm. Whatever he sees won't be comfortable, but it will be real.

The Watcher monitoring also changes with the delay. The coin in their possession rather than delivered means the crew is holding a piece of his history. He notices. The uncanny signs intensify slightly — not threatening, but more present.

Option C — They Consider Keeping It

This is the moment where the crew's trust in Scurlock becomes a live question. They've been inside the sanctum now. Alaric opened that door with his blood. Brick saw the Watcher's last dive. Handing this specific thing to Scurlock feels different from handing over the Ledger.

If they push back or refuse: Scurlock's mask slips slightly. Not dramatically — he is too composed for that — but the warmth drains out of Jeeves's next visit. The framing shifts from collaborative to transactional. "The arrangement requires mutual good faith."

This is a valid crew choice. It doesn't end the campaign — it accelerates the Scurlock betrayal arc and puts the question of who the crew is actually working for on the table earlier than planned. Let it happen if they go there.
