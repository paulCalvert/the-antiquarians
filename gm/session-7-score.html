Session 07

The Sunken Temple

Eight spaces. Two acts. Low stakes, high mood. Character moments for Brick, Flint, Victor, Alaric.

The Score — Eight Spaces

Space 1 — The Canal Mouth

Brick leads them through a canal passage to a submerged archway — old construction, pre-barrier, easy to miss. He knows it's there. The first wrong reflection: he sees it in the canal surface before they go under. A glimpse of something deeper than the water should be. He's the only one who catches it. Water already slightly heavier than it should be.

Challenge: None. Threshold only. The group enters on his knowledge.

Space 2 — The Outer Hall

Large vaulted space. Dockworkers have been coming here for generations — leaving coins, rope, fish bones, crude carved figures. Some offerings are recent. Nobody told them to. They just felt like they should. Harrow feels the ghost field immediately: old deaths, old grief, nothing hostile.

The city-spirit seed — describe going in: Central figure, arms raised overhead, palms facing upward, fingertips pointing outward. A presenting gesture: we hold this up to you, this is yours. Offerings clustered at its base. Face worn featureless.

If Harrow reads the ghost field going in: honor and supplication. Old, settled, sincere.

The puddle: Against the far wall, below the largest cluster of offerings. Six inches across, in a shallow depression. Dark, still water. Coins tossed in. Offerings placed as close as possible without touching. Nobody stands in it. It has looked like this for longer than anyone has been alive.

If anyone looks directly into it: it doesn't reflect the room. It reflects the open bay.

Brick tells them what he knows. That's everything he knows.

The commit: Not a roll. A choice. The first person who goes — describe their feet going through what looks like six inches of standing water and not stopping. Shin, knee, hip. Gone. The people still in the room watch.

Challenge: None yet. Exploration, tone, the commit moment.

Space 2 — On the Retreat

Call back to the statue. Same arms raised, palms still upward. The fingertips now point backward — toward the body, toward where a face should be. The change is small enough to miss if they're moving. When anyone looks directly: both positions visible simultaneously, a shimmer between the two, neither fully resolving.

If Harrow reads the ghost field now: fear and the unknown. The city doesn't have language for what changed. It only knows something did. Don't name it. Don't explain it.

Space 3 — The Open Bay

The puddle takes them through. Below: nothing. The floor of the bay is so far down it isn't visible. Open dark water going deeper than any of them can process. The Watcher's presence makes every cubic foot heavier than it should be. The gravity of this place is the deep. The natural motion is down.

The collapsed wall is visible above and ahead. There is a gap in it. That is the way through. It is not far. It is entirely possible to make it. The water is working against that.

Challenge: Resolve, risky. The physical distance is manageable. The problem is looking down.

  • 6: Through. They kept their eyes on the wall. Exhausted. Intact.
  • 4–5: Through, but they looked down. Made it, functional — but whatever they saw in the dark below is sitting somewhere behind their eyes. 1 stress. They don't describe it to the others.
  • 1–3: Started going down instead of up. Someone already through has to come back — which means going back into it. Or: they made it, but they saw something. Not the Observer. Something vast and still and patient, far below, looking up. They surface and don't speak for a moment.

Brick goes first — he knew about the puddle, he goes first. Wrong-water sense at full volume. He surfaces on the other side and waits.

Harrow should not look down. He looks down.

Flint — no mechanism, no system, no problem he can solve with his hands. Just open nothing and the need to swim.

Space 4 — The Watch Chamber

Larger vaulted room, several passages meeting. Some side passages collapsed. Old tool marks — someone was excavating here and stopped. Pre-Cataclysm construction in the ceiling vaulting. Victor notices the technique — unusual, older than the standard.

Challenge 1 — The Observer: Drops from the ceiling without warning between them and the forward passage. Starfish-cephalopod, ink-dark, arms that move wrong. One enormous eye. Does not advance. Watches. Leaves an impression: you are being catalogued.

  • Attune (Harrow): On success — monitor, not predator. Reports to something vast. It is filing them away.
  • Resolve (everyone else): Hold steady. Failure: flinch, noise, drawn weapon — it flows into a crack. More agitated for the rest of the score.
  • If attacked: Gone before the blow lands. Cannot be fought.

Challenge 2 — Structural instability: One forward passage is actively shifting — water pressure, old stone giving way. Wreck/Tinker to shore it up; Prowl to slip through before it closes. Worse if the Observer is agitated.

Space 5 — The Pump Corridor

Descending passage. Colder. Air heavier, wetter. Against one wall: remains of a mechanical drainage pump — hand-operated, rope-and-pulley, still bolted into the stone. It ruptured. Not from age: from overpressure, someone pushing it too hard trying to clear the passages ahead. Brown staining on the stone. Blood, long dried.

Someone died here trying to get through to the door.

The ghost echo: Harrow sees it, or anyone who looks closely. A figure in their last moments — not trying to escape, not calling for help. Trying to work a mechanism. Hands moving over the place where the pump housing was, working a lock or valve that no longer exists. The explosion destroyed it. The echo doesn't know that. Still trying to fix the problem, working the phantom mechanism, over and over.

For everyone: strange, sad, unsettling.
For Flint: someone trying to undo damage that can't be undone, working a system past the point where the system can help — lands somewhere personal. He probably doesn't say anything. He probably brings it up later. Let him.

Challenge: None. Breathing room. Move through at whatever pace the table sets.

Space 6 — The Door Chamber

Wide chamber. Back wall: sealed door — heavy, pre-Cataclysm, no visible mechanism. Six house seals carved into the arch above it. Three intact. Three cracked through. In front of it: broken picks, cracked hand drills, fresh gouges alongside old ones. One or two Ash Compact cultists camped here. Bedroll, cold food, research notes on the floor. Cipher marks on their gear.

Challenge — The cultists:

  • Prowl — slip past. Hard with a group.
  • Sway / Consort — talk them down. Territorial, paranoid, but not fighters. Days of failure have worn them thin.
  • Command — stand down. Fast. Creates an enemy.
  • Skirmish — loud, messy, disturbs the Observer.

Before the encounter resolves — Alaric sees the arch. One of the six seals is a tree. More detailed than his crest, more leaves, the trunk textured and full — but unmistakably the same symbol he's had on documents and signet rings his whole life. He's been looking at a simplified echo. The people who simplified it didn't have a living reference anymore. He's looking at what his family was actually remembering.

Whatever happens with the cultists: Alaric touches the stone — nothing. Blood: thumb against the sharp edge of the carving. The door opens. Silently. As though it was never closed. Play this slowly. The cultists see it happen after everything they tried. They react — lunge, freeze, beg. The crew gets through. The door closes. Cultists cannot follow.

Space 6 — Cultist GM Notes

Not fighters. Independent cell — don't know Scurlock's name ("let not one hand know the work of the other"). Days of failure. Research notes show they knew the ward required something inherent, not mechanical — they just couldn't figure out what.

Victor's moment — Dirmot's tools: The specialized drilling equipment bears a maker's mark Victor recognizes: Dirmot's workshop. His betrayer has been supplying equipment to Setarra's people. He says nothing. He looks at the mark for a moment and keeps moving. Leave ambiguous whether Dirmot knows who he's supplying.

On the flood: When water seeps back through from Space 8, the cultists run. You won't need to manage them.

The Sanctum — Two Rooms

Space 7 — The Equipment Chamber

First room beyond the bloodline door. Relatively dry. No human has been here since before the Cataclysm. Different air. A quality of silence the outer temple doesn't have.

The Observer follows through the closing door impossibly — flows through the gap like something that doesn't fully obey space — and settles high on the wall. Eye open. It will not leave.

At the far end: heavy stone door, no lock — just centuries of disuse and settled weight. Flint reads the seam and mechanics in about thirty seconds and gets it open. Clean, quiet, efficient. No drama. That's who he is. What was seeded in Space 5 pays off here — he just does the thing.

  • Victor: Full pre-Cataclysm diving suit on the wall. He reads it — pressurization systems, material seams, joint construction. Then: the fabrication technique echoes the Hull parts from the train. Not identical. Ancestral. Like whoever built the first Hulls was working from a template no one remembered came from here. He doesn't say it out loud. He files it.
  • Brick: The helmet. He looks into the faceplate. Not his reflection — the bay, clear and alive, lit from above as though the sun still exists. A figure in a diving suit descending into it, growing smaller. The last dive. The man before he became the thing. Hold this beat. Don't rush it.
  • Alaric: Six seals at the base of each wall, one per face of the hexagonal chamber. Three intact, three cracked. The Dalmorrian seal here is the most complete version he will ever see — a full tree, branches spread wide, leaves individually carved, the trunk detailed and textured. His modern crest is a stripped-down echo, simplified by generations who no longer had a living tree to reference. In Doskvol, trees are mythological. His family has been carrying a memory of the world before the sun went out, and here is what they were actually remembering. The stone is warm to his touch. He opened this room with his blood and the room knows it.

Challenge: None. Discovery and character texture. Don't rush.

Space 8 — The Bay Chamber

Ankle to shin deep across much of the floor — but it doesn't move like ankle-deep water. The Watcher's presence saturates it. Every step is the resistance of waist-deep water. Cold. Heavy. The room is working against them just by existing.

At the far edge: the open bay. The water simply stops — a vertical wall of dark seawater from floor to ceiling held back by nothing visible. Beyond it, the bay goes down into black nothing. No barrier, no glass, no mechanism. The Watcher's will.

Just beyond the membrane, on a humble moss-covered pedestal: the coin. Close enough to reach. Bone dry. The Observer is in the water. Just behind the membrane, watching.

Crossing the floor: No roll unless someone rushes or panics — but describe the effort. Every step deliberate. Brick feels it most. Something enormous just registered their footsteps.

Taking the coin: No roll. Someone reaches through the membrane — cold beyond cold, a threshold. The coin crosses back. Dry. The membrane fails. The bay comes in. Not a wave — just water, rising from the far wall, steady and patient. And it is thick.

Space 8 — The Flood (Resistance Track)

Don't count segments. Track it as a feeling that builds.

  • Early — manageable: Ankle-deep but heavy. Like wading through wet sand. They can grab items, move with purpose.
  • Mid — difficult: Shin-deep, each step a fight. Grabbing something from Space 7 is a real cost.
  • Late — dangerous: Struggling to stay upright. Moving more than a few feet requires a roll.
  • Full — impossible: Anyone still in Space 8 cannot get out without desperate action.

Retreat: Push back through Flint's door into Space 7. Flint pulls it shut. Stone on stone. Water presses against it — and holds. Bay stays in Space 8. Once through, water is normal again.

Items: Anyone who wants to grab something from Space 7 must do so against the rising resistance. Victor and a piece of the diving apparatus. Let them make the choice.

The Observer stays in the water as they retreat. Perfectly still in the thickening bay, at home in everything that's trying to hold them back. That eye, watching them go. Last image of the sanctum as the door closes.

The End Beat

Surfacing

They come up into the harbor district. Cold air. The city is still there. The coin is in someone's pocket, dry and unremarkable. Nothing dramatic has happened yet.

Then Brick feels it — the water beneath the docks shifts. Not a wave. Not a current. Something vast and old, patient as stone, just turned its head and looked directly at them for the first time.

The Watcher knows.

End the session there.

The City Spirit — One Seed Only

The central statue in Space 2. Describe going in: arms overhead, palms up, fingertips pointing outward. On the retreat, call it back: same pose, same palms, fingertips now pointing backward. Both positions visible simultaneously when looked at directly. Don't name it. Don't explain it.

Ghost field: honor and supplication going in; fear and the unknown coming out. The city has been tending the compact through the dockworkers' folk behavior — shrines, the Tide Tithe, the puddle nobody stands in. It knows something changed.

The Watcher — Context

Pre-Cataclysm bay diver. Tossed a coin before every dive. Was submerged when the Cataclysm hit; a deep demon merged with him. Still bound to protect the bay. The Tide Tithe is an echo of his personal ritual. The coin is his last toss — the one he never made. He makes water thick and heavy — the closer to his domain, the stronger. He is not yet hostile to the crew. After tonight, he is interested.
